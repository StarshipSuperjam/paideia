## Non-obvious finding A — `engine/STATE.md` carries two contradictory ADR-count claims, both wrong

**The fact.** [`engine/STATE.md`](../../engine/STATE.md) line 11 (the
Current row of the headline table) reads *"54 ADRs — 52 Accepted +
2 Superseded — 19 engine + 35 product."* Line 75 (the Strong working
commitments section) reads *"ADR count post-S-0045 (corrected at S-0052
audit): 52 ADRs total — 50 Accepted plus 2 Superseded ... partitioned
into 17 engine + 35 product."* Both are wrong, and they disagree with
each other on the total (54 vs 52) and the engine count (19 vs 17).

Mechanical census at this audit:

```
$ ls engine/adr/[0-9]*.md | wc -l    # 21
$ ls product/adr/[0-9]*.md | wc -l   # 35
$ # Total: 56 ADRs
$ # Superseded: ADR 0002 (by 0032) + ADR 0032 (by 0035) → 2
$ # Accepted: 56 − 2 = 54
```

The script-generated report independently produced "ADR collection: 54
accepted, 2 superseded" — agreeing with the mechanical census post-fix.
So the correct numbers are **56 ADRs total — 54 Accepted + 2 Superseded —
21 engine + 35 product**. Line 11 was under by 2. Line 75 was under by 4
(preserved verbatim from the S-0052 audit's correction sentence; the
four post-S-0052 engine ADRs — 0052, 0053, 0054, 0055 — were never
folded into the line-75 narrative even though each was added in a session
that touched STATE.md).

**Why the validator didn't catch it.** [`engine/tools/validate.py`](../../engine/tools/validate.py)'s
`adr_index_consistency` check audits the **ADR-file-vs-index alignment in
the two README files** (`engine/adr/README.md` and `product/adr/README.md`).
Both READMEs are correct (manually verified). What `adr_index_consistency`
does NOT check is **prose ADR counts in STATE.md or other narrative
documents**. STATE.md's two count-bearing sentences sit outside the
audit's reach.

This is a structural pattern worth naming: **the validator audits
structured surfaces (READMEs as machine-readable indexes) but not
narrative surfaces (STATE.md prose claims about counts)**. STATE.md is
read at every session boot; an ADR-count claim there is high-trust by
position. The S-0052 audit caught the same drift class — its line-75
sentence even *names* the prior drift (*"the pre-S-0052 number (51 / 16
engine) was drift — S-0045's ADR addition didn't propagate"*). The
sentence that catches the drift then accumulates the same drift over the
next 25 sessions. **Meta-pattern: a narrative claim that the validator
can't audit will drift even when the document containing it is written
by sessions that know better.**

**Disposition.** Two parts:

1. **Fixed inline this session.** STATE.md line 11 → "56 ADRs — 54
   Accepted + 2 Superseded — 21 engine + 35 product." Line 75 →
   prepended an S-0077 amendment line acknowledging the drift +
   updated census, appended the missing post-S-0052 ADRs (0053, 0054,
   0055) to the additive narrative, and added a posture note to the
   end of the section pointing future edits at the re-count requirement.
2. **Accept the structural gap (posture, not machinery).** Adding
   STATE.md prose-claim audits to `validate.py` is over-engineering;
   STATE.md prose is AI-authored, not a mechanical contract. The right
   corrective is **author-discipline-at-edit-time**: any STATE.md edit
   touching the headline-table "Current phase" row or the Strong
   working commitments section *triggers a re-count*. Posture, not
   machinery. The amendment to STATE.md line 75 itself records this.

## Non-obvious finding B — `mode` field absent on S-0069 archive; structured-field-consistency gap extends prior Finding A

**The fact.** [`engine/session/archive/S-0069.json`](../../engine/session/archive/S-0069.json)
does not carry the `mode` key at all (the field is absent, not `null`).
The `mode` field was added per [ADR 0051](../../engine/adr/0051-routine-mode-and-engine-loop.md)
to discriminate routine-mode from interactive sessions; downstream
audits read it to characterize windows. The same class also affects
[S-0065.json](../../engine/session/archive/S-0065.json) (outside the audit
window, but the prior audit's own archive — wry).

The archive shape is otherwise complete: `outcome_summary_soft_warns` is
present (per the ADR 0042 audit at S-0055), `scope_delivery` is
populated, `closed_at` is populated. Only `mode` is missing.

**Cross-reference with prior audit's Finding A.** S-0065's Finding A
named [`engine/session/archive/S-0057.json`](../../engine/session/archive/S-0057.json)
as a slip-through of `outcome_summary_soft_warns` from a structured-field
defense ([`engine/tools/audit_archive_structured_fields.py`](../../engine/tools/audit_archive_structured_fields.py)
guards `outcome_summary_soft_warns`, not `mode`). The prior audit named
the deeper pattern as *"audits firing only at the choke point detect the
next lapse, not the prior lapse — detecting the prior lapse needs a
separate scan, and we didn't build it."* This audit's S-0069 finding
**confirms the pattern repeats with a different field**:

| ADR adds field | Audit covers it? | Field-absence pattern emerged | Caught at |
|---|---|---|---|
| ADR 0042 → `outcome_summary_soft_warns` | Yes (S-0055) | S-0057 | S-0065 |
| ADR 0051 → `mode` | **No** | S-0069 (and S-0065) | S-0077 |

The structural problem is identical: every new structured field that
ADRs add to archives accumulates the same defense gap *unless* the
defending audit is generalized to the field set rather than to one
specific field at a time. Issue [#20](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/20)
(filed S-0065) names the archive-history-scan extension; that
extension is the exact instrument this finding wants. Without #20
closed, every new structured field is a future structured-field-absence
finding waiting to be discovered by the next audit.

**Disposition.** Three parts:

1. **No retroactive backfill of S-0069 / S-0065.** Same posture as
   S-0065's S-0057 disposition — editing a historical archive's
   structured field after the fact is editing the record-of-record.
   Forward-going machinery treats absence as default; the lapse stays
   in the archive as record-of-failure-mode.
2. **Update Issue [#20](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/20)
   with the new evidence.** The pattern now has empirical evidence of
   repeating with a different field; the cost-benefit analysis for
   closing it strengthens. Cross-session deferral per
   [ADR 0048](../../engine/adr/0048-handoff-narrowing-and-github-issues-for-cross-session-deferrals.md);
   the cleanup-batch surface picks it up.
3. **Posture cue (extends S-0065 Finding C).** The next time an ADR
   adds a new structured field to archives, **explicitly extend the
   defending audit (`audit_archive_structured_fields.py`) to cover the
   new field in the same session**. The "no row, no authoring" pattern
   from [`expression-contract-instantiation.md`](../../engine/operations/expression-contract-instantiation.md)
   has the right shape; extending the audit becomes part of the new
   field's first-exercise gate per [ADR 0053](../../engine/adr/0053-mechanism-first-exercise-gate.md).
   Annotation, not action this session.

## Non-obvious finding C — `transcript_token_pct` recovered from 9/10 None to 6/11 None without anyone fixing Issue #21

**The fact.** Issue [#21](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/21)
was filed S-0065 against `transcript_token_pct = None` in 9/10 archives
(post-ADR-0049). In the new audit window (S-0066 → S-0076):

```
S-0066 None        S-0072 None
S-0067 None        S-0073 None
S-0068 0.3476      S-0074 0.3879
S-0069 None        S-0075 0.4384
S-0070 0.3925      S-0076 0.3406
S-0071 None
```

5 of 11 captured (45%). The prior audit had 1/10 captured (10%). Capture
rate **improved 4.5× without anyone touching `scan_context_telemetry.py`
or Issue #21** (the issue is still open and unmodified since filing).
The sessions that captured don't share an obvious feature: routine
sessions on both sides (S-0068/S-0070/S-0074/S-0075/S-0076 captured;
S-0066/S-0071/S-0073 didn't). The lone interactive session in the
window (S-0072) didn't capture. No mode-stratified explanation, no
temporal ordering, no apparent worktree-vs-main-repo discriminator.

**Why this is non-obvious.** A metric that's silently broken can be
fixed (you know it doesn't work). A metric that's silently working can
be relied on (you know it works). A metric that **silently fluctuates**
between working and not-working is *worse than either* — its absence
might mean the tool failed today, or it might mean today's session
didn't qualify, and you can't tell from the field alone. Downstream
audits cannot reason about Session-load trends without a reliability
assumption, and the reliability is now empirically 45%.

The improvement from 10% → 45% is interesting — something changed in
the environment between S-0064 and S-0066 that improved the
filesystem-heuristic behavior at shutdown. Plausible candidates:

- The S-0072 routine-lifecycle-push parent-FF amendment per
  [ADR 0054](../../engine/adr/0054-lifecycle-push-wrapping-against-default-branch-push-gate.md)
  changed worktree state at session close in ways that may have
  stabilized which JSONL transcript file the heuristic picks.
- The Phase 5 routine sessions are short (median ~27 min per the
  script-generated report's archive-metadata aggregate), reducing the
  surface for the encoded-path drift the original heuristic was
  failing on.
- A Claude Code harness change between S-0065 and now may have made
  the projects-directory layout more predictable in routine context.

The audit doesn't attempt to diagnose definitively — that work belongs
to Issue #21, not this audit's scope.

**Disposition.** Update Issue [#21](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/21)
with the new capture-rate data and **the fluctuation observation as the
new core problem statement** — the issue's original framing was "62%
None"; the new evidence reframes the issue as "telemetry capture is
non-deterministic in ways the heuristic does not explain," which is
materially different and changes the candidate fix-set (heuristic
hardening probably won't help; the fix likely needs to be at-boot
path capture or moving the capture mechanism out of filesystem-heuristic
land entirely). Cross-session deferral per ADR 0048 — not load-bearing
for any pending phase, picks up at the next cleanup-batch surface.
