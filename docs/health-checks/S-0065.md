## Non-obvious finding A — S-0057's close bypassed the audit designed to prevent exactly this

**The fact.** [`engine/session/archive/S-0057.json`](../../engine/session/archive/S-0057.json)
carries no `outcome_summary_soft_warns` key (the field is absent, not
`null`, not `{}`). The same archive is the only post-S-0055 archive
shaped pre-shutdown — `status: "in_progress"`, `outcome_summary: null`,
`scope_delivery: null`, no `closed_at`. The archive was added in commit
`6813d28` ("close S-0057 — P5-03 logic seed lands"); from STATE.md and
the ROUTING entry, S-0057 was a successful Phase 5 routine session that
landed migration `0090_seed_logic_part1`. So the close *worked* (P5-03
flipped to complete, the deliverable migration applied, the next session
proceeded normally), but the close *commit's* archive content shape never
got the shutdown writeback.

**Why this is the audit's exact job.**
[`engine/tools/audit_archive_structured_fields.py`](../../engine/tools/audit_archive_structured_fields.py)
was added at S-0055 specifically to defend ADR 0042's persistent-warn
surface against silent field-absence lapses (the S-0043 → S-0047 pattern
identified by the S-0052 audit's Finding C). The pre-commit hook
([`engine/tools/hooks/pre-commit:229-246`](../../engine/tools/hooks/pre-commit:229))
detects closing-mode commits, locates the staged archive blob via
`git diff --cached --name-only --diff-filter=A`, and pipes it through the
audit. The audit hard-fails with exit 2 on a missing `outcome_summary_soft_warns`
key. Replayed against the staged content of S-0057's archive today, the
audit hard-fails as expected (verified against `git show 6813d28:engine/session/archive/S-0057.json`).
The mechanism is correct; the wiring was correct at S-0055's close (commit
`808eb9a` lands the audit; S-0056 closes 2h later with the field present;
S-0057 closes 38m after that without it).

**What the audit can't see.** Per the audit script's docstring, *"No
archive-history audit. This fires only against the in-flight current.json.
Retroactively detecting lapses across past archives belongs in
health_check.py."* The S-0055 design accepted that the audit defends only
the close-time choke point, on the explicit theory that one slip-through
becomes invisible after the close commit — exactly what happened with
S-0057. Subsequent persistent-warn-surface reads of "the last 5 archives"
silently includes S-0057 in the calibration window with the field absent;
the surface degrades gracefully (treats absence as 0) but the data is
still missing.

**Root cause not definitively identified.** No `--no-verify` usage exists
anywhere in the project's lifecycle code (grepped: only two prose mentions,
both explicit prohibitions). All recent close commits (S-0055 → S-0064)
share the same diff-status shape (`A engine/session/archive/...` + `D
engine/session/current.json`), the same author, the same hook installation
(symlink `/Users/shanekidd/Documents/Claude_Files/Paideia/.git/hooks/pre-commit
→ ../../engine/tools/hooks/pre-commit`). What changed only at S-0057?
Unknown without forensic reconstruction. Plausible candidates: a transient
worktree-state anomaly that misclassified MODE detection at hook entry; a
race where the staged archive blob differed from on-disk during the audit
read; a crash mid-shutdown that re-attempted close from cached state.
Forensic root-cause is rabbit-hole work that doesn't change the fix.

**Disposition.** Two parts:

1. **Defer S-0057 archive backfill.** Editing a historical archive's
   structured field after the fact is editing the record-of-record. The
   forward-going machinery does not need the backfill; persistent-warn
   surface treats absence as 0. The lapse stays in the archive as a
   record of the failure mode for any future audit to re-examine.

2. **File [Issue #20](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/20)**
   for a `health_check.py --archive-history` extension that, at audit
   time, walks all archives and reports those missing
   `outcome_summary_soft_warns` (or other ADR 0042 / ADR 0049 structured
   fields). Cross-session deferral per ADR 0048 — the next health-check
   session boot can pick it up; the work is auditing-tool extension,
   not load-bearing for any pending phase. Tracked-as-issue.

The deeper machine lesson is that audits firing only at the choke point
detect the *next* lapse. Detecting the *prior* lapse — which is what we
actually need when the choke audit slips — needs a separate scan, and we
didn't build it. ADR 0042's amendment record (the audit-script docstring's
"Out of scope") explicitly named this gap. We accepted the gap then; the
gap activated within two sessions of acceptance.

## Non-obvious finding B — `transcript_token_pct` is silently None in 15 of 24 archives since ADR 0049

**The fact.** Of the 24 archives written since ADR 0049 wired
`transcript_token_pct` capture at S-0042 (boot of which was the second
health-check session), the field is `None` in 15 — including S-0048,
S-0049, S-0050, S-0054, S-0055, S-0057, S-0058, S-0059, S-0060, S-0061,
S-0062, S-0063, S-0064. The fraction is 62%; the trend over the last 10
archives is 9 of 10 None. The 9 archives that captured a value cluster
around interactive sessions (S-0042, S-0043 → S-0047, S-0051, S-0052,
S-0053, S-0056) and one routine-mode session (S-0056); the routine-mode
era (S-0057+ excluding S-0056) has captured 0 valid values.

**Why this matters.** The Session-load-trend section of every health-check
is supposed to read `transcript_token_pct` across the last N archives
and surface running too long / running too short / high variance per the
operative discipline section of [`engine/operations/health-check.md`](../../engine/operations/health-check.md).
With 9 of the last 10 sessions reporting None, the trend section cannot
fire — there are not enough non-null samples for the comparison. The
mechanism degrades silently into a section the audit cannot populate.

**Root cause.** [`engine/tools/scan_context_telemetry.py`](../../engine/tools/scan_context_telemetry.py)
reads `~/.claude/projects/<encoded-project-path>/<session-id>.jsonl`.
The harness does not expose the active session ID or transcript path to
the tool; the tool relies on filesystem heuristics. When the heuristics
fail (the encoded path varies between worktree boot and main-repo boot;
the session ID picker can't disambiguate when multiple JSONL files exist
in the projects directory; the file isn't yet flushed at shutdown step
7b), the tool writes None and the field carries None into the archive.
The tool's own docstring and Issue #4's closure (intractable per
CLAUDE.md "Posture vs machinery") acknowledge the heuristic fragility,
but the framing was about live-pressure-detection — what's failing here
is the cumulative-cross-session telemetry that should be more reliable.

**Disposition.** File [Issue #21](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/21):
investigate why `scan_context_telemetry.py` returns None in the routine-mode
case in particular. Two candidate fixes named (the audit doesn't pick
between them, but pre-flags the path):

- (a) Read `CLAUDE_TRANSCRIPT_PATH` or equivalent env var if it exists,
  fall back to heuristic when absent. (Requires checking what the harness
  actually exposes — claude-code-guide pattern.)
- (b) At session boot, capture the transcript path into `current.json`
  via a SessionStart hook step; at shutdown, read it back from the
  in-flight session JSON. Side-steps the at-shutdown filesystem-heuristic
  brittleness.

`tracked-as-issue`. Cross-session deferral per ADR 0048; not load-bearing
for any pending phase. The Session-load-trend section of *this* report
notes the data gap and proceeds without trend prose.

## Non-obvious finding C — meta-pattern: defenses we acknowledged-as-incomplete activate as gaps

S-0052's audit closed Issue #11 inline (the >1.0 `transcript_token_pct`
misread) and filed Issue #13 for the structured-field audit (which
S-0055 implemented as the audit S-0057 then bypassed). The pattern is:

| At-the-time decision | What activated |
|---|---|
| ADR 0042 audit covers in-flight only ("retroactive belongs in health_check.py") | S-0057 slipped through; no retroactive scan exists |
| Issue #4 closed as intractable for live-pressure detection | `transcript_token_pct` cumulative telemetry inherits the same brittleness |
| ADR 0049 captures `transcript_token_pct` upper-bound | The capture itself silently fails 62% of the time |

Each individual at-the-time decision was reasonable. The pattern across
them — *acknowledge a known gap; defer the gap-closer; the gap activates*
— is the audit's own warning to itself. There's no specific corrective
action for the meta-pattern; the corrective is naming it. The next
mechanism that ships with an explicit "out of scope" should be paired
with a tracked Issue for the out-of-scope item, not just a docstring
comment, so the gap is visible at every boot's backlog scan.

**Disposition.** Annotation, not action. The two specific Issues filed
above are the concrete corrective; the meta-observation is for the
next session (or audit) to remember.
