## Non-obvious findings (the audit's own observation; no scanner produces these)

### A. Routine-mode prerequisites surfaced one-at-a-time across S-0048 → S-0051; the build-readiness gate did not catch them

ADR 0051 landed at S-0044 with six layers of anti-rogue defense and a documented end-to-end procedure. Then four sessions in succession each surfaced and closed a different routine-mode infrastructure gap, each filed as an Issue with `priority:urgent`:

| Slot | Issue | Symptom |
|---|---|---|
| S-0048 | #7 | Phase 5 routines cannot execute: `SUPABASE_DB_URL` missing from interactive `.env` (dashboard-only password discoverability gap). |
| would-be-S-0049 (pre-claim bail) | #8 | `.env` is gitignored — not propagated to worktrees by `git worktree add`; tools read `os.environ.get` directly without `load_dotenv`. |
| S-0050 | #9 | `check_target.py` queried `WHERE version = %s` but `version` is the timestamp; auto_target.json `id` is the migration name. Verifier silently returned FAIL post-apply. |
| S-0051 | #10 | Boot-time has no worktree-staleness check; AI sees stale `register_state.json` / `current.json` / `auto_target.json` and proceeds on stale truth. |

**Composite reading: routine-mode was not actually ready for unattended dispatch at S-0044, even though ADR 0051's six-layer defense was authored at production quality.** Four real prerequisites were missing and surfaced one-at-a-time as the routine fired against real DB state. The build-readiness gate at S-0034 (per [ADR 0040](../../engine/adr/0040-build-readiness-gate-before-substantive-build-sessions.md)) was scoped to Phase 4 graph validation; the routine-mode session pattern got no equivalent pre-flight.

This isn't a story of negligence. Each gap is the kind of thing exhaustive a-priori analysis is unlikely to surface — the `.env` walk-up problem is invisible until you try a routine fire from a fresh worktree; the `check_target.py` column bug is invisible until a real migration lands. The honest reading is: **for novel cross-cutting mechanisms (routine-mode is one), a brief first-use pre-flight exercise — fire the mechanism once interactively against real state, observe end-to-end, close gaps — is value-positive even though the gaps would have surfaced anyway in slow motion.** The cost of S-0048→S-0051's serialized infrastructure-gap closure was four sessions of context, four Issue authoring rounds, and four routine pauses; a single first-use pre-flight at S-0045 would likely have caught all four (the pre-flight session itself surfaces the issues in scope).

The pattern generalizes beyond routine-mode. Worth surfacing as a tension or proto-ADR consideration. **Disposition:** filed as Issue #12 (`tech-debt`) — "Mechanism-first-exercise gate for novel cross-cutting mechanisms"; not urgent (the next mechanism of comparable scope isn't on the immediate roadmap), but worth deciding before the next routine-mode-tier mechanism lands.

Three of ten archives carry `pct > 1.0` — physically impossible for a live-pressure metric and visible at every shutdown of those sessions. The field is upper-bound cumulative-content (the docstring at [`engine/tools/scan_context_telemetry.py:20-24`](../../engine/tools/scan_context_telemetry.py) explicitly says so) and is suitable for cross-session "running too long / too short" judgment per the Session-load trend section below — but the *output line* the AI reads at shutdown reframes it as `pct=N.NNNN`, which reads as live-pressure semantics by adjacency to other live-pressure surfaces in the same stderr stream.

Issue #11's two proposed fixes (reframe the output line; CLAUDE.md "Posture vs machinery" bullet for the live-vs-cumulative distinction) are exactly right and small. **Disposition:** fixed-in-session at commit (see end of report); Issue #11 closed.

### C. The persistent-warn surface that ADR 0042 mechanizes was structurally unable to fire across half the audit window

Five of ten archives in this window — S-0043, S-0044, S-0045, S-0046, S-0047 — lack the `outcome_summary_soft_warns` key entirely (not just empty; the field is absent from the JSON):

```bash
$ for s in S-0042..S-0051; do jq 'has("outcome_summary_soft_warns")' archive/$s.json; done
S-0042: true | S-0043: false | S-0044: false | S-0045: false | S-0046: false | S-0047: false
S-0048: true | S-0049: true | S-0050: true | S-0051: true
```

Five consecutive sessions skipped the structured field. ADR 0042's persistent-warn surface (3-of-5 across recent archives) is calibrated against *structured* data — it cannot detect a missing field, only count category occurrences inside the field. So:

- The boot-surface message *"calibration window in effect (4/5 structured archives; surface defers until 5)"* is the visible symptom — the surface defers when fewer than 5 of the last 5 archives are structured.
- The mechanism cannot detect its own missing input. A session that forgets to write the structured field is invisible to the surface for the next 5 sessions.
- The shutdown procedure carries the discipline (writing the field is a step in [`engine/operations/session-shutdown-sequence.md`](../../engine/operations/session-shutdown-sequence.md)). The discipline lapsed for five sessions in a row, then reappeared at S-0048. Plausibly: S-0048's GitHub-Issues-cleanup session re-internalized the discipline; or the AI in those sessions skipped the step under perceived budget pressure (Finding B above is the same root cause if so — false-pressure read of `transcript_token_pct`).

**The structural fix is a shutdown-time field-presence audit.** A short script (`engine/tools/audit_archive_structured_fields.py`) reads `engine/session/current.json` after the shutdown sequence's structured-field write step and hard-fails (or soft-warns) if the field is missing. Symmetric to `audit_handoff_dispositions.py` per [ADR 0048](../../engine/adr/0048-handoff-narrowing-and-github-issues-for-cross-session-deferrals.md) — the shape is "audit a shutdown-step deliverable for presence." **Disposition:** filed as Issue #13 (`health-check-finding`) — "Structured-soft-warns field-presence audit at session shutdown"; in-session implementation would expand audit scope substantially (new tool + tests + ops-doc wiring) so cross-session deferral via Issue is the right pattern, but the Issue is concrete and actionable.

## Session-load trend (per ADR 0049, with the Finding B caveat)

`transcript_token_pct` across the audit window has 3 entries above 1.0 (S-0044, S-0046, S-0047) — those values are not interpretable as live pressure (Finding B). For the cross-session trend purpose the field *is* designed for:

- **Running-too-long signal:** 3+ of last 5 archives above 0.60 → signal would fire at 3-of-5 in S-0042..S-0046 (S-0042=0.81, S-0044=1.11, S-0045=0.62, S-0046=1.62 — 4 of 5 above 0.60). But the >1.0 reads don't have stable mappings to "long" — they could mean a session that genuinely ran long *or* a session with dense tool-result content (lots of file reads expand the transcript without expanding live prompt). Both explanations fit the routine-mode foundation work in S-0044 and the dual-session-on-one-worktree pattern in S-0046/S-0047.
- **Running-too-short signal:** none below 0.30 in the populated entries.
- **High variance:** stddev across populated entries is large (the >1.0 outliers contribute), but the metric being upper-bound makes variance hard to interpret.

3 archives carry `null` for the field (S-0048, S-0049, S-0050) — telemetry capture skipped. Reasons unknown (the script exits 0 with a stderr note when transcript is unfindable; the archived JSON inherits null). Worth noting but not actionable from this audit.

**Recommendation:** the field is more legible as a cross-session "did this session author a lot, or a little" indicator than as a pressure metric. After Finding B's output-line reframing lands, the cross-session use should hold up. If the >1.0 reads cluster in sessions that *did* author heavy content, the metric is doing genuinely useful work for trend purposes — just not the false-pressure interpretation that Issue #11 caught.
