## Non-obvious finding(s)

### Non-obvious finding A — The `mempalace_hnsw_divergence` soft-warn is invisible to per-session archive telemetry

**Observation.** The boot probe (`validate.py --health-probe-only`, invoked by `session-start.sh`) runs the `mempalace_hnsw_divergence` check and emits the soft-warn category when the HNSW index diverges from SQLite ground truth. THIS audit's boot probe (timestamp `2026-05-12T21:22:05.999281Z` in `engine/tools/validate-history.jsonl`) recorded `"mempalace_hnsw_divergence":1,"chromadb_palace_health":1`. The full in-session `validate.py` invocation (timestamp `2026-05-12T21:26:03.392892Z`, session_id `S-0141`) **does not list `mempalace_hnsw_divergence` in its `checks_run` array** — only `shared_state_palace`, `shared_state_repo`, `shared_state_session_dir` survive into the full validate. Per ADR 0042, `outcome_summary_soft_warns` is populated from the full validate (pre-commit hook scope), not from the boot probe. Result: a session's archive carries a record of MemPalace HNSW health **only if the LOUD attention surface caught the AI's attention at boot** — there is no structural carrier into the canonical archive. The Probe 1 contradiction (probe_palace says `healthy`, search reports 94% invisible) is the same shape: separate surfaces, no consolidation.

**Why it's non-obvious.** The boot probe LOOKS load-bearing: it's documented in `health-check.md` "Maintenance probes" (added at S-0084 per Issue #31), it has a soft-warn category, it has a LOUD attention threshold (≥30% divergence). But ADR 0042 made the structured archive the canon for soft-warns, and the boot probe never made it into the structured archive. The two ADRs (0042 + 0084-era probe additions) don't compose at the archive layer. A session can boot with `mempalace_hnsw_divergence:1` in the boot-probe log, run a full session, close cleanly, and produce an archive where soft-warn telemetry shows no MemPalace concern at all.

**Recommendation.** Either: (a) extend the full in-session `validate.py` checks_run set to include `mempalace_hnsw_divergence` (so the soft-warn flows into archived `outcome_summary_soft_warns` per ADR 0042 canon); or (b) extend `scan_mempalace_activity.py` / archive shutdown machinery to read the boot-probe jsonl and merge boot-probe-only soft-warns into archive telemetry. Option (a) is cheaper and more aligned with the existing canon. **File as `health-check-finding` Issue.**

### Non-obvious finding B — `issue_collision` is firing 19/19 sessions with effectively zero acted-on signal, but the persistent-warn 10-session escalation criterion was never tripped

**Observation.** Per ADR 0042's persistent-warn lifecycle, soft-warns firing in 3-of-5 archives surface at boot and ≥10-session persistence escalates. `issue_collision` has fired 19/19 sessions in the audit window — far past 10 sessions. The category has accumulated 22-23 collisions per session (median across window). The acted-on rate is 1/19 (a phase-move from structural to health-probe phase, not a false-positive-rate fix). **No promotion-or-retirement decision has been made.** The escalation criterion was never tripped because — per `tools-validate-interpretation.md` — `issue_collision` is annotated as expected-baseline.

The annotation is the failure mode. The annotation says "this is expected because we have many open Issues whose body terms match common session scopes." That's true mechanically, but it converts a high-volume signal into a silent channel. A real false-positive-rate fix is possible: the check could exclude trigger-gated Issues (e.g., #79, #82, #83, #84, #85 all carry `[TRIGGER: ...]` in their titles), upstream-blocked Issues (#1, #2), and obviously trigger-gated future-phase work — that alone would drop the median from 23 to ~5 and restore the check's signal-to-noise. Issue #62 ("Add 4 validator soft-warns from S-0122 audit closeout proposals") is still OPEN per a S-0122 audit recommendation — a year of audits has flagged validator improvements and at least one is queued indefinitely.

**Why it's non-obvious.** Audit machinery looks correct (persistent-warn surfacing works, ADR 0042 canon is held, archive telemetry captures the counts). But the annotation channel is a **legitimate-exception escape hatch that, once used, becomes invisible to all downstream audits.** The S-0086 audit lesson ("preserve-with-reason is structurally cheaper to write than argue this should be retired") applies here — `issue_collision` got annotated and stayed annotated.

**Recommendation.** Either: (a) refine the `issue_collision` check to skip trigger-gated and upstream-blocked Issues by parsing their labels — would drop median from 23 to ~5 and let real signal surface; or (b) demote the check to a `--verbose` flag and remove from default output (acknowledges the noise; preserves the data for opt-in inspection); or (c) execute Issue #62's pending 4 validator soft-warns first, which may absorb the issue_collision concern. **File as `health-check-finding` Issue** if (a) or (b) is wanted; close Issue #62 first if (c) is preferred.

**Observation.** The audit window covers a three-session OSS+BYOK pivot (S-0128 / S-0129 / S-0130 — Apache 2.0, BYOK refactor, 141-file PII path sweep), Tier 1 + Tier 2 SWE-hardening closure (8 ADRs), multiple first-exercise readiness gates, the S-0140 premise correction (Issue #86 body said "add `revert:` to validate.py" but validate.py doesn't validate commit subjects — the audit-session narrowed the scope correctly), and the ADR-0077 introduction of "Alternatives Considered" as a template section. The S-0078 mechanical adoption check is doing its job. Either: (a) the drawers were written but their content is not query-relevant to the search terms I used; (b) the HNSW degradation prevents semantic recall from finding the audit-window drawers; or (c) the drawers being written are increasingly thin gloss (lifecycle-required) rather than substantive (lesson-capturing).

**Why it's non-obvious.** The S-0078 fix (ADR 0056) mechanized the diary-write requirement. Mechanization caught the silent-skip class (the S-0078 vector pattern). It cannot catch the **gloss-vs-substance** class — a session can write a thin diary that satisfies the mechanical check but doesn't capture the load-bearing pushback or lesson. **Recommendation.** Hand-sample 3-5 in-window archives' `add_drawer_calls` content (read the drawers each session wrote) and judge substance: do they capture push-back-worthy moments or are they routine? If the latter, the convention needs to surface explicit prompts for `pushback` / `lesson` capture beyond the diary-write step (the session-shutdown-sequence already has yes/no asks at step 7, added at S-0041 per the lesson drawer). ### Pushback clusters

**Cluster 1 — wrapper / harness-gate interactions.** Pushback drawers across the audit window (and earlier — the S-0061 / S-0060 / S-0024 cluster) demonstrate that wrapper-vs-documented-allowlist drift is a recurring class. The pattern: a project tool's hardcoded allowlist is supposed to mirror a documented allowlist in another file; the two drift; the drift surfaces only at first exercise. ADR 0053 (mechanism-first-exercise gate) was authored to catch this; it has partially worked (e.g., S-0138's `build_lifecycle_push.py` first-exercise gate, S-0140's negative-finding for `revert:` adoption).
