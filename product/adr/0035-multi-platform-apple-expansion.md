# ADR 0035 — Multi-platform Apple expansion; iPhone + iPad first-class via SwiftUI, Mac via Designed-for-iPad

- **Status:** Superseded by [ADR 0065](0065-oss-pivot-and-byok-disposition.md)
- **Supersedes:** [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md)
- **Date:** 2026-04-30
- **Deciders:** S-0015 (third session of Phase 1.5; lands the platform-scope supersession that [ADR 0033](0033-mission-realignment-structured-guidance-for-self-learners.md) named in its Consequences and that the editorial / typographic / library-shaped product structure following from [ADR 0034](0034-discovery-planning-engagement-triad.md)'s triad makes natural); superseded in S-0128

> **Superseded 2026-05-11 (S-0128).** [ADR 0065](0065-oss-pivot-and-byok-disposition.md) revises this ADR's disposition for the OSS pivot plus BYOK shift. Commitments 1 and 6 preserve verbatim (Apple platforms via App Store + iPhone / iPad first-class via SwiftUI + Mac via Designed-for-iPad; static polish + minimum-shape maintenance). Commitments 2 and 4 invert: free download with a one-time IAP unlock replaces the cost-priced subscription (commitment 2); consumer BYOK is the required regime (commitment 4) — institutional BYOK, institutional licensing, grants, and acquisition remain foreclosed unchanged. Commitments 3 and 5 retire: the builder no longer eats API costs (users pay Anthropic directly through their own accounts); the cost-ceiling mechanism per [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md) (also superseded in S-0128) is structurally absent under BYOK. Two new commitments: source open under Apache 2.0 with attribution to "The Paideia Project Contributors" (commitment 7); the user's API key lives only on-device in iOS Keychain, the server never sees the key or proxies the API exchange (commitment 8). The corruption-vector test from [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) — preserved through this ADR — continues to bind: the OSS+BYOK shift removes the cost-recovery surface entirely, re-shaping the funnel-mechanic threat from *builder*-mechanic to *user*-mechanic without weakening the discipline against it. The institutional-foreclosure half of commitment 4 is explicitly preserved. The historical reasoning below preserves per the ADR README's supersession discipline (one-directional pointer; old file not deleted).

## Context

[ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) committed Paideia to **single-platform iOS via App Store** (commitment 1) as part of a six-commitment personal-project disposition that foreclosed the institutional / non-profit / grant / acquisition option set as a refusal, not a deferral. The narrowing was structural-not-tactical: any future session proposing Android or web would have to supersede ADR 0032, paying the supersession discipline as the cost of reopening the platform question.

[ADR 0033](0033-mission-realignment-structured-guidance-for-self-learners.md) (S-0013) and [ADR 0034](0034-discovery-planning-engagement-triad.md) (S-0014) changed the product structure underneath that platform commitment without changing the disposition spirit. Two threads in the post-realignment design make the single-platform-iOS narrowing the wrong shape:

**The product structure that follows from the globe drop is editorial, typographic, library-shaped.** ADR 0034's Discovery surface (browseable concept catalog organized by domain and topic, AI conversational onboarding) and Planning surface (syllabus-as-plan with prerequisite ordering, current/active syllabus view, mastered concepts visible in syllabus context) are screen-real-estate-friendly affordances. A browseable catalog of philosophy / theology / history / literature concepts works well at the size of a paperback or a small-format book; it works less well in a phone column. A syllabus showing prerequisite ordering, current position, and a sequence of upcoming concepts has natural information density that a phone screen taxes uphill. The Engagement surface's bring-your-own-book close reading per [ADR 0011](0011-no-hosted-copyrighted-material.md) — a learner working through Hegel or Wittgenstein with the AI instructor against the book in front of them — wants the page open and the reading visible at a real-text size; phone-only reading is a worse register for the kind of contemplative work the system is designed for. The triad does not foreclose phone use; it reveals that **the phone is naturally a follow-up surface for content the user has already encountered on a larger screen**, not the primary surface where the heavy work happens.

**Apple's "Designed for iPad on Mac" path makes Mac distribution essentially free.** Apple Silicon Macs run iPad apps from the App Store by default unless the developer explicitly opts out (`UIRequiresFullScreen` and similar entitlements being absent). A single SwiftUI codebase targeting iPhone + iPad — which is the natural shape of a SwiftUI app already, given size-class adaptive layout and the iPad's status as a first-class iOS platform — produces a Mac binary as a side effect of the iPad target, distributed through the same App Store with the same review path and the same Apple Small Business Program take rate. The marginal cost of "Mac via Designed-for-iPad" over "iPhone + iPad" is small: modest keyboard polish (Mac users expect Cmd+arrow navigation, Cmd+W to close, Cmd+, for settings), menu bar items where Mac idiom calls for them, and window-resize handling consistent with macOS behavior. The cost of *not* offering Mac is larger than it appears, because Apple Silicon Macs are exactly the device where philosophy reading benefits most from the screen real estate the product structure rewards.

**The disposition spirit that [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) committed survives the broadening intact.** ADR 0032's six commitments addressed the corruption vector: "leave the option open to expand later" was the seed pattern that pulled design toward business outcomes pedagogy never warranted on its own. The threat ADR 0032 identified was *builder*-funnel-mechanic-touching-pedagogy: building for a market — even a market the builder believes deserves the product — pulls attention toward retention and conversion in ways pedagogy would not warrant. **None of that threat changes with the platform broadening.** Android requires a separate codebase, separate review queue, separate billing surface, separate maintenance burden — exactly the kind of multi-platform commitment that turns a personal project into a small business. A web app introduces a third operational surface (web hosting, browser compatibility, distinct billing path) and a fourth distribution channel (search-engine-optimization-oriented surfaces that pull toward funnel mechanics by their structural shape). A native AppKit Mac app duplicates the engineering surface against a platform that already runs the iPad codebase. The Apple-platform broadening is **single-codebase, single-store, single-billing-path**: one SwiftUI codebase, one App Store, one In-App Purchase surface, one review queue, one privacy questionnaire, one refund window. The maintenance shape stays minimum-shape. The refusal-not-deferral commitment preserves at the new boundary: Android, web, and native AppKit Mac are each foreclosed; reopening any one of them requires superseding *this* ADR, with the same supersession discipline ADR 0032 committed against the original narrowing.

The forcing function is timing. The supersession lands in S-0015 (this session) rather than later because Phase 9 is the implementation phase that consumes the platform commitment, and Phase 9 success criteria are rewritten in this same session. Authoring the supersession ADR after Phase 9 success criteria already locked in iOS-only language would force a second rewrite. The reasoning chain ([ADR 0033](0033-mission-realignment-structured-guidance-for-self-learners.md) → [ADR 0034](0034-discovery-planning-engagement-triad.md) → ADR 0035) is internally connected: the editorial / library-shaped product structure that follows from the globe drop motivates the platform broadening; the platform broadening shapes the Phase 9 implementation surface; Phase 9 carries both forward as one consistent contract.

## Decision

**Paideia ships via the Apple App Store on Apple platforms. iPhone + iPad first-class via a single SwiftUI codebase. Mac via Designed-for-iPad opt-in with modest keyboard/menu polish. No Android, no web app, no native AppKit/Catalyst Mac app. The personal-project disposition's other five binding commitments survive verbatim from [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md).**

The disposition has six binding commitments. Commitment 1 broadens; commitments 2–6 restate.

**(1) Apple platforms via App Store. iPhone + iPad first-class via a single SwiftUI codebase. Mac via Designed-for-iPad opt-in with modest keyboard/menu polish.** Distribution is the Apple App Store — not sideload, not TestFlight-only at steady state. iPhone and iPad targets share one SwiftUI codebase; size-class adaptive layout handles the form factor differences. Mac distribution is via the "Designed for iPad" opt-in (Apple Silicon Macs run iPad apps from the App Store by default unless explicitly opted out). The Mac polish target is *modest, not Mac-native*: Mac-idiom keyboard shortcuts (Cmd+arrow, Cmd+W, Cmd+, etc.), menu bar items where the surface naturally calls for them (e.g., File → New Syllabus, View → Active Syllabi, Window → standard idioms), window-resize handling consistent with macOS, and a polish pass on the focus-ring and pointer-hover behaviors the iPad codebase gets for free under iPadOS pointer support. Mac is *not* a first-class platform in the sense of a native AppKit/Catalyst app with full macOS idiom — that would duplicate the engineering surface and forfeit the single-codebase discipline. Apple Developer Program enrollment ($99/yr) covers all three targets. The DeepTutor fork (Apache 2.0) is consulted only for what serves the Apple-native target. The narrowing is structural: if a future session proposes Android, web, or native AppKit Mac, the proposal must supersede this ADR; the cost it pays is the supersession discipline.

**(2) Cost-priced subscription via Apple In-App Purchase. No free tier.** *Restated verbatim from [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) commitment 2.* Target $12.99/mo monthly with an annual subscription option at the typical 15–20% discount. Apple's Small Business Program (15% take rate under $1M annual revenue) is presumed. The App Store screenshots and the 7-day Apple refund window function as the trial affordance — a separate free-tier UX is *not* engineered, because a free tier is the canonical builder-funnel-mechanic that pulls pedagogy toward retention and conversion ceremony. Pricing language remains learning-meaningful, not vendor-SKU-meaningful. The single tier is structurally necessary for the no-funnel-mechanic discipline; tiering by learning intensity is foreclosed at this disposition's scale.

**(3) Builder eats API costs within a fixed annual operating subsidy budget. No grants, no 501(c)(3), no institutional licensing.** *Restated verbatim from [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) commitment 3.* The builder's personal subsidy budget — order-of-magnitude $300/year for infrastructure (Apple Developer Program + VPS + domain), plus the marginal API cost of the builder's own use — covers the operating floor. Paid users at $12.99/mo cover their own marginal API at the price point's design margin. The subsidy budget is *not* expected to fund free-tier API consumption (no free tier exists) and is *not* expected to scale with user count. The numeric value of the budget remains private operational configuration consistent with [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md)'s discipline.

**(4) No bring-your-own-key, neither consumer nor institutional. No institutional regime, period.** *Restated verbatim from [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) commitment 4.* OQ-BYOK-REGIME remains closed by foreclosure (its institutional candidate excluded by the no-institutional-regime commitment; its consumer candidate self-selects technically sophisticated users away from the freshman audience the system is calibrated for per [ADR 0012](0012-freshman-defaults-autodidact-ceiling.md)). The institutional schema provisions previously dropped from `docs/architecture.md` at S-0012 stay dropped; this ADR does not reopen the institutional question. Syllabus upload may survive as a personal-use feature; its sales-wedge / institutional-onboarding framing does not.

**(5) [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md) reframed as fixed annual operating subsidy budget — nothing ever raises it.** *Restated verbatim from [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) commitment 5.* The cost-ceiling principle ADR 0029 commits is unchanged in mechanism (per-user cap, aggregate cap, real-time spend telemetry, defined cap-behavior, soft-walls-degrade-don't-terminate). What ADR 0032 changed in the framing of the ceiling's value — fixed annual operating subsidy budget, not bridge-to-grants — survives this ADR unchanged. **If this scales beyond what the personal subsidy budget supports, the response is to raise prices to slow growth or to cap signups, not to pursue institutional clients, grants, or acquisition.** This sentence is the contract; the platform broadening does not relax it. Multi-platform reach increases the surface where users discover the app, which increases the importance of the per-user cap mechanism doing its job; the broadening does not change what "doing its job" means.

**(6) Polish is static, never dynamic. Maintenance is minimum-shape, deliberately.** *Restated verbatim from [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) commitment 6.* The polish target is "as polished as solo + AI can manage" along static surfaces only — visual design, typography, animation, copy quality, layout, iconography, sound design, haptics. Dynamic-feature surfaces are explicitly foreclosed: no social, no sharing, no leaderboards, no streaks, no push notifications beyond auth/billing, no community, no in-app messaging, no "what's new" surfaces. Maintenance posture is minimum-shape up front: no live support email surface (FAQ-only support); release cadence at builder discretion (4–8 week typical baseline); the builder is not a customer service department. **The "modest keyboard/menu polish" for Mac in commitment 1 above is consistent with this static-polish discipline** — keyboard shortcuts, menu bar items, focus-ring polish, and pointer-hover behaviors are static-surface concerns, not dynamic features.

**Success criterion: "An app I would pay for if it weren't mine."** *Restated verbatim from [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md).* This is a self-applying criterion: the builder is the test, and the builder's bias toward "knowing how the system works" obscures whether the system is usable cold. The builder-bias failure mode is what keeps two existing ADRs load-bearing for new reasons: [ADR 0012](0012-freshman-defaults-autodidact-ceiling.md) (freshman defaults — calibration for a learner encountering ideas for the first time *cold*) and [ADR 0027](0027-rendering-policy-prompt-layer-contract.md) (rendering policy worked example — the gradeable test artifact at Phase 7) protect against builder-bias drift. The Phase 9 small-cohort cold-test (2–3 people who have not seen the project, given the build with no instructions) is the verification artifact, not an ongoing program. Cold-testers may use any of the three Apple targets; the builder picks the form factors the cohort tests on based on what's available.

## Consequences

- **`ROADMAP.md` Phase 9 success criteria are substantially rewritten in this session.** The current Phase 9 success criteria (set at S-0008, narrowed at S-0012 by [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md)) reference globe-as-home-screen + concept-engagement-surface + iOS-only-native + Android / Google Play exclusion. The rewrite replaces the globe-as-home-screen + concept-engagement-surface framing with the Discovery / Planning / Engagement triad per [ADR 0034](0034-discovery-planning-engagement-triad.md); restates the platform target as iPhone + iPad first-class via SwiftUI, Mac via Designed-for-iPad; drops "iOS/Android primary, web test surface" framing entirely (Android already foreclosed, web foreclosed, the prior framing's surviving fragments serve no purpose). The commitments preserved unchanged: cost-cap mechanism on production user-facing surface (per [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md)), no general chat surface (per [ADR 0028](0028-input-side-scope-structural-not-prompt.md)), exit affordance as first-class UI primitive (per [ADR 0028](0028-input-side-scope-structural-not-prompt.md)), delete-account affordance (per [ADR 0031](0031-erasure-mechanism-and-individual-only-regime.md)), data-export affordance (per commitment 6 inherited unchanged into this ADR via [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md)), static-polish discipline, dynamic-feature foreclosure.

- **`docs/business.md` Pricing and Distribution Distribution row is amended in this session.** The Distribution row becomes: "Apple App Store. iPhone + iPad first-class; Mac via Designed-for-iPad." The Apple Developer Program row ($99/yr) is unchanged. Other rows — Pricing model, Monthly target, Annual discount, Take rate, Trial affordance, BYOK foreclosure, Pricing language — are unchanged; the disposition spirit of [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) commitments 2–6 is preserved verbatim and the table remains coherent under the broadened commitment 1.

- **[ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) Status flips from `Accepted` to `Superseded by ADR 0035` in this session.** The historical reasoning preserves per the ADR README's supersession discipline (one-directional pointer; old file not deleted). The corruption-vector identification ADR 0032 carried — "leave the option open to expand later" was the seed pattern that pulled design toward business outcomes pedagogy never warranted — survives in this ADR's restatement of commitments 2–6 and remains the discipline against which future supersessions are tested. The ADR 0032 file gains a supersession callout box (analogous to the one ADR 0002 received) explaining the broadening: this is supersession-by-narrowing-revision, not supersession-by-reversal — five of six commitments restate verbatim; commitment 1 broadens within the same disposition spirit.

- **The Mac polish target is modest, not Mac-native, by design.** The discipline is single-codebase, single-store. Investing in a native AppKit / Catalyst Mac app would duplicate the engineering surface against a platform that already runs the iPad codebase. The Mac polish entries — Mac-idiom keyboard shortcuts, menu bar items, window-resize handling, focus-ring / pointer-hover polish — are within scope; full macOS idiom (multi-window with state restoration, document-based architecture, Mac-native preferences, custom menu bar beyond the iPad-app default) is out of scope. A future session proposing the Mac-native treatment must supersede this ADR with explicit reasoning for why the duplicated engineering surface is justified.

- **The single-SwiftUI-codebase commitment is structural.** Sharing the codebase between iPhone, iPad, and Mac (via Designed-for-iPad) is what makes the broadening minimum-shape per commitment 6. A future session proposing a separate codebase for any Apple target — for example, a separate iPhone-only build branched off for a phone-optimized rendering pass — must supersede this ADR. Size-class adaptive layout, conditional `if #available` blocks for platform-specific affordances, and the natural iOS/iPadOS divergences SwiftUI handles transparently are within scope.

- **The Phase 9 small-cohort cold-test (per [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md), inherited unchanged) may consume any of the three Apple targets.** A 2–3 person cohort is too small to systematically cover all three form factors; the builder picks the test surface based on what cold-testers' available devices are. The cold-test is a verification artifact for the success criterion ("an app I would pay for if it weren't mine"), not a multi-platform QA pass.

- **Apple Developer Program lead time is unchanged.** The 2–4 week enrollment window per [`docs/business.md`](../docs/business.md) Pricing and Distribution covers all three Apple targets. Phase 8 retains it as a forcing function. The privacy policy and Apple App Store privacy questionnaire (per ROADMAP Phase 8 success criteria) cover all three targets in one consumer App Store posture; no DPA / FERPA framing.

- **[ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md)'s mechanism is unchanged; its operating-discipline framing tightens.** Multi-platform reach increases the surface where new users discover the app, which increases the importance of the per-user cap doing its job; the broadening does not change what the cap is for. The reframing committed at S-0012 (fixed annual operating subsidy budget, not bridge-to-grants) survives this ADR. ADR 0029 is not superseded.

- **[ADR 0031](0031-erasure-mechanism-and-individual-only-regime.md)'s erasure mechanism is unchanged.** Hard-delete with cascade applies identically across all three Apple targets — the schema is platform-independent. Apple App Store guideline 5.1.1 (in-app account deletion) is satisfied at the schema level for the iPad-app build that runs on Mac via Designed-for-iPad just as it is for the iPhone build.

- **The corruption-vector test ADR 0032 recorded generalizes to this ADR.** "Leave the option open to expand later" remains the seed pattern future sessions are tested against. *Does this proposal preserve optionality whose value depends on a pedagogical or operational direction the builder no longer commits to?* If yes, supersede or foreclose. The Apple-platform broadening passes the test: it broadens the *pedagogical reach* of the editorial / library-shaped product structure to the screen sizes that structure rewards, without preserving optionality whose value depends on retention/conversion mechanics. Android, web, and native AppKit Mac do not pass the test under the same disposition; they remain foreclosed.

- **The refusal-not-deferral commitment is binding at the new boundary.** The most likely failure mode of a refusal-style commitment is silent re-litigation when growth makes the refused option attractive. This ADR's binding form: *if a future session would propose Android, web, or native AppKit Mac, the proposal must supersede this ADR with explicit context for why the disposition has changed.* Reopening requires the supersession discipline; per-session re-litigation is not authorized.

- **Cancellation discipline is unchanged.** The data-export affordance per [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md) commitment 6 (preserved unchanged here) is the user-facing capability for cancellation; cascade-delete per [ADR 0031](0031-erasure-mechanism-and-individual-only-regime.md) is the user-facing capability for erasure. Both surfaces are first-class UI primitives required for Phase 9 close on every Apple target. The Apple Developer Program enrollment lapses if not renewed annually; this remains acceptable under the cancellation discipline.

- **This commitment does not rise to [`docs/MISSION.md`](../docs/MISSION.md)'s strong-working-commitments list.** Consistent with the [ADR 0026](0026-persistent-learner-storage-structural-not-substantive.md), [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md), [ADR 0031](0031-erasure-mechanism-and-individual-only-regime.md), [ADR 0032](0032-personal-project-disposition.md), [ADR 0033](0033-mission-realignment-structured-guidance-for-self-learners.md), and [ADR 0034](0034-discovery-planning-engagement-triad.md) precedent: this is operating discipline (platform scope), not a pedagogical commitment. The audience-facing pedagogical commitments survive in MISSION.md commitments 1–12 unchanged; commitment 2's reframing at S-0012 ("Operating discipline must not corrupt pedagogy") absorbs the platform-scope discipline this ADR commits, in the same way it absorbs the disposition discipline ADR 0032 committed. No MISSION.md edit is required by this ADR.

- **The MemPalace decision drawer for this ADR carries the conversational reasoning verbatim.** The S-0015 reasoning that connected the post-realignment product structure to the Apple-platform broadening — the editorial / typographic / library-shaped surfaces wanting larger screens; the "Designed for iPad on Mac" path being essentially free distribution; the disposition spirit surviving the broadening intact — is recall-by-similarity content for future sessions facing analogous "should we broaden the platform scope" choices. The drawer is filed in the same session per the two-layer decision-recording discipline in CLAUDE.md.

- **Phase 1.5 advances by one session.** Phase 1.5.1 (mission realignment + globe/reward closure) closed at S-0013 with [ADR 0033](0033-mission-realignment-structured-guidance-for-self-learners.md). Phase 1.5.2 (Discovery / Planning / Engagement triad) closed at S-0014 with [ADR 0034](0034-discovery-planning-engagement-triad.md). This session closes Phase 1.5.3.
