# ADR 0031 — Erasure mechanism (hard-delete with cascade); individual-only data regime

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-04-30
- **Deciders:** S-0011 (privacy ADR session; enabled by an exploration-session commitment that immediately precedes this session and is queued for formal supersession in S-0012)

## Context

[ADR 0026](0026-persistent-learner-storage-structural-not-substantive.md) settled the privacy *posture* (persistent learner storage is structural, not substantive) and opened two sub-questions to be settled before Phase 3 schema authoring: **OQ-PRIVACY-A** (the erasure mechanism — crypto-shredding vs. hard-delete-with-cascade vs. anonymize-and-aggregate) and **OQ-PRIVACY-B** (the institutional vs. individual data regime, direction-neutral). Both questions sit in [`docs/tensions.md`](../docs/tensions.md) as Open / decide-before-Phase 3.

Three of the candidates in OQ-PRIVACY-A's three-way trade space had non-trivial weight when [ADR 0026](0026-persistent-learner-storage-structural-not-substantive.md) was written. **Crypto-shredding** is attractive for an event-sourced architecture with an institutional audit-trail value: a per-learner KMS key is deleted on erasure, events remain in the table but become permanently unreadable, the [ADR 0015](0015-event-sourced-learner-model.md) audit-trail shape is preserved. **Hard-delete-with-cascade** is the simplest and most reliable mechanism and loses the audit trail. **Anonymize-and-aggregate** preserves graph-level signal at the cost of a contested fit with GDPR Article 17 — at consumer scale, event sparsity makes "anonymized" residuals potentially re-identifiable.

OQ-PRIVACY-B's direction-neutrality was deliberate: institutional cohorts arriving via a Data Processing Agreement might be *more* eligible for aggregate analytics than individuals (ToS-clickthrough), or *less* eligible under FERPA-style stricter regime. The right call depended on which direction the project actually moved.

The S-0011 conversation that preceded this build session settles the project direction. Paideia ships as a **single-platform iOS App Store consumer subscription app, cost-priced, with no institutional regime, no bring-your-own-key, no enterprise wrapper, no grants, no 501(c)(3), and no acquisition-exit path.** "Leave the option open" is closed as a refusal, not a deferral, including in the success case. The supersession ADR that retires [ADR 0002](0002-commercial-sustainability-without-pedagogical-compromise.md) and substantively rewrites [`docs/business.md`](../docs/business.md) and [`docs/MISSION.md`](../docs/MISSION.md) is the next session's work (S-0012). This ADR lands ahead of that supersession because the privacy ADRs were the active Phase 1 work and they collapse cheaply once the project-direction commitment is in hand.

The consequence for OQ-PRIVACY-A: two of the three candidates lose their motivating force. Crypto-shredding's audit-trail-preserving virtue is a virtue *for institutional contractual reasons*; with no DPAs and no institutional analytics, no party needs to read aggregate analytics that include erased users post-hoc. Anonymize-and-aggregate's graph-level-signal virtue is similarly institutional in shape — at consumer scale the graph-level signal contribution from any single learner's events is negligible. What survives is hard-delete-with-cascade, which has always been the simplest and most reliable mechanism — and which best satisfies Apple App Store guideline 5.1.1's user-initiated data-deletion requirement.

The consequence for OQ-PRIVACY-B: the question is moot. There is no institutional regime to design for and no analytics-eligibility distinction to make. The institutional schema provisions in [`docs/architecture.md`](../docs/architecture.md) (`cohort_id` nullable, shareable constrained paths) become dead-weight; their removal is S-0012's work, but [ADR 0026](0026-persistent-learner-storage-structural-not-substantive.md)'s sub-decision (2) — the structured-context columns on `learner_events` — should not include `cohort_id`.

## Decision

**Settle OQ-PRIVACY-A as hard-delete with cascade. Withdraw OQ-PRIVACY-B as moot.**

**(1) Erasure mechanism: hard-delete with cascade.** When a user requests account erasure (or operationally requires it), the durable user data is removed by `DELETE FROM users WHERE id = ?` with `ON DELETE CASCADE` foreign-key constraints carrying the deletion through to `learner_events`, `mastery_snapshots`, and `tension_log` rows linked to that user. Cascade discipline is authored as part of the Phase 3 schema migration. Apple App Store guideline 5.1.1 (in-app account deletion) is satisfied by a user-facing affordance that calls this cascade; the affordance itself is Phase 9 UI work.

The audit-trail value the event-sourced architecture (per [ADR 0015](0015-event-sourced-learner-model.md)) preserves — the ability to inspect or recompute a user's history after the fact — is *deliberately not preserved through erasure*. Erasure means erasure. Mastery snapshots derived from a user's events are also deleted on cascade because they would be stale and meaningless without the underlying events. Cohort-aggregated analytics are not affected because no cohort-aggregated analytics exist in the surviving project shape.

**(2) Institutional vs. individual regime: individual only.** The project has no institutional regime. [ADR 0026](0026-persistent-learner-storage-structural-not-substantive.md) sub-decision (2)'s `learner_events.context` shape is amended in this ADR: the structured columns are `path_id`, `source_text_id`, and `session_id`. **`cohort_id` is removed.** Phase 3 schema authoring proceeds without an institutional column reservation. The institutional schema provisions in [`docs/architecture.md`](../docs/architecture.md) (the `cohort_id` field, shareable-constrained-paths-as-institutional-wedge framing) are dead-weight whose removal lands in S-0012 alongside the supersession ADR and the broader docs rewrite.

**(3) Two rejected candidates from OQ-PRIVACY-A are recorded as rejected.** *Crypto-shredding* is rejected: per-learner KMS keys add operational complexity (key generation, rotation, backup, recovery) that earns its keep only when an audit-trail-preserving erasure is contractually required. The project will not have such contracts. *Anonymize-and-aggregate* is rejected: the graph-level signal contribution from a single user's events at consumer scale is negligible, and event sparsity makes residual data potentially re-identifiable — both the upside and the GDPR Article 17 fit are weak.

## Consequences

- **Phase 3 schema gains `ON DELETE CASCADE` foreign-key discipline** across `learner_events`, `mastery_snapshots`, and `tension_log`. The exact column-and-FK shape is Phase 3 migration work; this ADR commits to the cascade discipline at the principle level. The simplest shape: `learner_events.user_id` as a foreign key with `ON DELETE CASCADE` to `users.id`; `mastery_snapshots.user_id` likewise; `tension_log` linked via `session_id` to a session record that carries `user_id`, or directly via `tension_log.user_id` with `ON DELETE CASCADE`. The Phase 3 migration session selects the concrete shape and tests the cascade end-to-end.

- **`learner_events.cohort_id` is removed from the [ADR 0026](0026-persistent-learner-storage-structural-not-substantive.md) sub-decision (2) shape.** The structured columns are now `path_id`, `source_text_id`, `session_id`. The institutional column reservation that motivated `cohort_id` no longer exists. Adding it back would require a superseding ADR that reopens the institutional regime.

- **Apple App Store guideline 5.1.1 is satisfied at the schema level.** The Phase 9 UI affordance ("delete my account") wires to a server endpoint that runs the cascade; the schema makes the cascade reliable. This is a precondition for App Store submission and aligns with the Phase 8 privacy-policy success criterion in [ROADMAP.md](../../ROADMAP.md).

- **The audit-trail value of event-sourcing is retained for non-erasure use cases.** [ADR 0015](0015-event-sourced-learner-model.md)'s discipline still governs in-flight learners: events are the source of truth, mastery snapshots are derived. Hard-delete-with-cascade only fires on erasure requests, which are rare and irreversible. Day-to-day operation is unchanged.

- **Internal fine-tuning** (per [`docs/business.md`](../docs/business.md), Internal fine-tuning from session data) inherits a cascade implication: if a user's events are deleted before their structural patterns have entered a fine-tuning corpus, that user's contribution is lost. This is acceptable — the cost-mitigation framing in [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md) does not require any specific user's data, only aggregate patterns. Fine-tuning corpora are built from snapshots taken at fixed times, not from a live event stream that can disappear.

- **OQ-PRIVACY-A and OQ-PRIVACY-B move out of `docs/tensions.md`.** OQ-PRIVACY-A is settled by this ADR; OQ-PRIVACY-B is withdrawn by this ADR. The Phase 2 → Phase 3 boundary check in [`docs/CROSS_REFERENCES.md`](../docs/CROSS_REFERENCES.md) is satisfied with this session's close. Phase 1 substantively closes at S-0011; Phase 1 fully closes at S-0012 once the supersession ADR retires [ADR 0002](0002-commercial-sustainability-without-pedagogical-compromise.md) and the docs rewrites land.

- **This ADR depends on a project-direction commitment that is not yet formally recorded.** The S-0011 conversation settled the commitment; the S-0012 supersession ADR is the artifact. If S-0012 surfaces a complication that reopens the institutional regime, this ADR may require revision (the cascade-only erasure shape would still likely survive — institutional contracts can mandate audit-trail preservation, but the simplest path to that mandate is a superseding ADR adopting crypto-shredding for institutional accounts only, leaving consumer accounts on hard-delete). The risk is judged low because S-0012's commitment was explicitly settled in user-directing form within S-0011's exploration. The contingency is named here so that any future session reading this ADR cold sees the dependency.

- **The cost-cap mechanism interaction is unchanged.** [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md)'s cost-ceiling discipline is independent of the erasure mechanism. Cap behavior, telemetry, and degradation ladder are unaffected by this ADR's choice. (Note: in S-0012's supersession, [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md)'s framing will be updated — cost ceiling reframed as a fixed annual operating subsidy budget rather than a bridge-to-grants — but its mechanism survives intact.)

- **This commitment does not rise to [`docs/MISSION.md`](../docs/MISSION.md)'s strong-working-commitments list**, consistent with the [ADR 0026](0026-persistent-learner-storage-structural-not-substantive.md) and [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md) precedent: erasure mechanics are operating discipline, not pedagogical commitment. Cross-references from [`docs/tensions.md`](../docs/tensions.md), [`docs/CROSS_REFERENCES.md`](../docs/CROSS_REFERENCES.md), and the Phase 3 entries in [ROADMAP.md](../../ROADMAP.md) are sufficient.

- **The cascade is reliable, not reversible.** A user who deletes their account loses their mastery state. The product surface should communicate this clearly at the deletion confirmation step (Phase 9 UI work). There is no soft-delete or 30-day-grace-period mechanism in this ADR; if a future session decides such a mechanism is warranted, it lands as a superseding ADR.
