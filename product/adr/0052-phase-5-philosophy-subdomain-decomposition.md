# ADR 0052 — Phase 5 philosophy-subdomain decomposition

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-05-04
- **Deciders:** S-0045 (Phase 5 master plan session per [ADR 0051](../../engine/adr/0051-routine-mode-and-engine-loop.md))

## Context

Phase 5 of the project authors a concept-level seed graph across philosophy subdomains per [ROADMAP Phase 5](../../ROADMAP.md). The phase plan landed in S-0001 as part of the original ROADMAP and was instantiated into [`build_plan/P_4_seed_graph_build.md`](../../build_plan/P_4_seed_graph_build.md). Both surfaces decomposed philosophy into six subject subdomains (epistemology, ethics, metaphysics, philosophy of mind, philosophy of language, philosophy of science) plus a service-nodes pass plus a cross-domain edges pass. The decomposition was a working list at the time of authoring; it was not adjudicated against how curated philosophy reference works (SEP, IEP, Routledge, Oxford) actually organize the discipline.

S-0044's close text in [`engine/STATE.md`](../../engine/STATE.md) (the "Next session work item" entry for S-0045) named a different decomposition: epistemology, ethics, metaphysics, **logic**, philosophy of mind, philosophy of science, **political philosophy**, **aesthetics** plus cross-bridges plus closeout. The two surfaces — the canonical build_plan and the post-S-0044 STATE.md text — disagreed on three additions (logic, political philosophy, aesthetics), one removal (philosophy of language), and the framing of service nodes (subsumed-or-removed in STATE.md, standalone in build_plan).

S-0045 is the Phase 5 master plan session per [ADR 0051](../../engine/adr/0051-routine-mode-and-engine-loop.md): an interactive `/start-engine` session whose deliverables are an executable contract ([`engine/session/auto_target.json`](../../engine/session/auto_target.json)) consumed by routine-mode sessions and a human-readable rationale doc ([`engine/build_readiness/phase_5.md`](../../engine/build_readiness/phase_5.md)). Authoring those deliverables requires a single, settled subdomain list. The contract layer needs to ratify the decomposition before the executable contract is authored — silent absorption of the divergence would violate [ADR 0049](../../engine/adr/0049-scope-lock-at-boot-and-descope-reorder-audit-at-shutdown.md)'s no-descoping-or-reordering clause and leave a long-running drift between canonical surfaces.

The adjudication compared each candidate decomposition against four criteria:

- **How SEP and Routledge organize the discipline.** Both reference works carry top-level browse categories that include logic, political philosophy, aesthetics, and philosophy of religion as standalone subdomains, separate from ethics and metaphysics. The build_plan's omission of those three made it materially less aligned with the structural-reference class established by [ADR 0046](0046-structural-reference-posture-extends-to-philosophy-reference-works.md).
- **Internal coherence.** Logic burying inside service nodes alongside math primitives and history terminators conflated philosophical logic (modality, conditionals, paradox, vagueness, paraconsistent, deontic) with formal-logic scaffolding. The two have different concept inventories and different graph-shape relationships to the rest of the discipline.
- **Coverage of major analytic-tradition concepts.** Philosophy of language is the home subdomain for sense/reference, speech acts, descriptivism, semantic externalism, and much of mid-20th-century analytic philosophy. STATE.md's omission of philosophy of language would have left those concepts homeless or forced them into philosophy of mind or epistemology where they don't structurally belong.
- **MVP scope discipline.** A first philosophy seed needs reasonable completeness — visible gaps in core subdomains feel incomplete to learners. Adding three subdomains (logic, political, aesthetics) over the canonical plan is substantial; subtracting philosophy of language is materially worse than that.

## Decision

Phase 5 decomposes into **9 subject subdomains plus 3 operational tasks**:

**Subject subdomains** (each receives ≥1 migration during routine-mode execution, may pre-split at master-plan time per [`engine/session/auto_target.schema.md`](../../engine/session/auto_target.schema.md)):

1. **Epistemology** — knowledge, justification, belief, certainty, skepticism, social epistemology
2. **Metaphysics** — being, identity, modality, causation, time, free will, properties, universals
3. **Logic** *(philosophical logic)* — modality, conditionals, paradox, vagueness, paraconsistent, deontic, intuitionistic, relevance. Formal-logic primitives (propositional, first-order, set-theoretic foundations) belong to the service-nodes task.
4. **Ethics** — metaethics, normative ethics (virtue, deontological, consequentialist), applied ethics
5. **Political philosophy** — authority, justice, rights, liberty, equality, democracy, social contract
6. **Aesthetics** — beauty, art, taste, expression, representation, interpretation, criticism
7. **Philosophy of mind** — consciousness, mental causation, intentionality, qualia, perception, personal identity, AI/computational mind
8. **Philosophy of language** — meaning, reference, sense, speech acts, descriptivism vs. causal theory, semantic externalism, vagueness in natural language
9. **Philosophy of science** — scientific method, explanation, confirmation, theory choice, realism vs. anti-realism, philosophy of physics/biology/social sciences

**Operational tasks:**

10. **Service nodes** — formal-logic primitives (propositional, first-order, set theory), mathematical prerequisites for cross-domain dependencies (arithmetic, algebra, calculus, probability), history terminators (concepts that anchor influence-edges but do not themselves bear the granularity-principle one-mastery-state property). Per [ADR 0008](0008-concept-nodes-not-thinkers.md) thinkers are not nodes; "history terminators" here means concepts at the historical-prerequisite cliff (e.g., "Ancient Greek thought" as a domain anchor, not "Plato" as a node).
11. **Cross-bridges** — cross-domain edges spanning subdomain boundaries per [ADR 0007](0007-cross-domain-porosity.md). Authored after subject subdomains stabilize so cross-domain edges target real concepts. Predicate registry expansion (`cross_domain_dependency` formal introduction) decided in this task per the manifest's existing reserved-but-unused convention.
12. **Closeout** — Phase 5 close: ENGINE_LOG entry, STATE.md update, `engine/build_readiness/phase_5_closeout.md` summary, optional close ADR if a Phase-5-wide finding warrants one.

**Out of scope, deferred:**

- **Philosophy of religion.** Historically a philosophy subdomain (cosmological argument, problem of evil, divine attributes, religious epistemology); SEP carries it as a top-level category. Deferred from Phase 5 because (a) cosmological-argument-shaped concepts can fold into metaphysics for an MVP without distortion, (b) the subdomain is sensitive territory for a single-author seed pass, and (c) post-Phase-5 expansion has clear admit-criteria via this ADR's class-boundary clause. Revisit when the project's maturity makes the additional coverage net-positive against the authoring burden.
- **History of philosophy as a separate subdomain.** Per [ADR 0008](0008-concept-nodes-not-thinkers.md), thinkers are not nodes. Historical concepts surface across the 9 subdomains via the cross-bridges pass — Kant's epistemology, Kant's metaphysics, Kant's ethics, Kant's aesthetics each live in their respective subdomain, with influence-edges between them surfaced in cross-bridges. There is no separate "history of philosophy" subdomain in this decomposition.
- **Non-philosophy domains.** History, theology, literature, economics, poetry, political theory, law, psychology — all named in the project's expansion plan but separate from Phase 5. Phase 5 is the philosophy seed; non-philosophy domains are post-Phase-5 expansion targets per [ROADMAP](../../ROADMAP.md).

**Class-boundary clause** (parallel to [ADR 0046](0046-structural-reference-posture-extends-to-philosophy-reference-works.md)'s admit-criterion): A future Phase 5 routine-mode session may surface a tenth subject subdomain whose coverage warrants standalone status (e.g., philosophy of religion, philosophy of mathematics as distinct from logic, phenomenology as distinct from philosophy of mind). That admission lands as a small ADR in the consuming session, citing the criterion (top-level coverage in SEP/Routledge browse categories AND graph-shape distinctness from existing 9 subdomains AND non-trivial concept inventory at the granularity principle). The decomposition is settled at 9 for now; the principle is stable; admission is per-case.

## Consequences

- **Single source of truth lands at this ADR.** [`ROADMAP.md`](../../ROADMAP.md) Phase 5.1 is updated in the same session to reflect the 9-subject decomposition. [`build_plan/P_4_seed_graph_build.md`](../../build_plan/P_4_seed_graph_build.md) sub-sessions list and success criteria are updated. [`product/seed-graph/migrations/ROUTING.md`](../../product/seed-graph/migrations/ROUTING.md) prefix scheme is extended (new ranges for language `0070-0079`, science `0080-0089`, logic `0090-0099`, political philosophy `0100-0109`, aesthetics `0110-0119`; Phase 6 self-correction relocated from `0070-0079` to `0120-0129`). [`engine/STATE.md`](../../engine/STATE.md) is updated at session shutdown to reflect post-S-0045 state. The build_plan vs STATE.md drift is closed.
- **Routine-mode dispatches against this ADR's task list.** [`engine/session/auto_target.json`](../../engine/session/auto_target.json) authored in this session encodes the 16 explicit tasks (epistemology, ethics, metaphysics, philosophy of mind each pre-split into core/specialized halves per [`auto_target.schema.md:149`](../../engine/session/auto_target.schema.md) "Break work into session-sized tasks" discipline; logic, political, aesthetics, philosophy of language, philosophy of science kept as single tasks; plus service nodes, cross-bridges, closeout). Subsequent routine-mode sessions execute this contract.
- **Migration prefix ranges are reserved per [ROUTING.md](../../product/seed-graph/migrations/ROUTING.md).** New ranges keep the existing 10-slot-per-subdomain convention. Phase 6 (self-correction schema additions) relocates from `0070-0079` to `0120-0129` — a one-time shift that costs nothing operationally because Phase 6 has not yet authored any migrations. Future per-subdomain authoring writes into the reserved range; cross-subdomain compound-domain concepts continue to use the higher-precedence subdomain's range per the ROUTING.md compound-domain handling rule.
- **Structural-reference posture extends naturally.** [ADR 0046](0046-structural-reference-posture-extends-to-philosophy-reference-works.md) governs SEP/IEP/Routledge/Oxford/Wikipedia consultation for graph-shape value across all 9 subdomains. No subdomain falls outside the posture; no subdomain requires a new structural-reference admission. Per-subdomain reference inventories from the [Phase 4.5 survey](../docs/content-strategy.md) "Cross-Domain Reference Inventories — Survey" carry over.
- **Predicate registry remains conservative.** [`product/seed-graph/migrations/PREDICATE_MANIFEST.md`](../../product/seed-graph/migrations/PREDICATE_MANIFEST.md) currently commits two predicates (`pedagogical_prerequisite`, `historical_influence`); reserves but does not use `cross_domain_dependency`. The cross-bridges task (P5-11) decides whether to formally introduce `cross_domain_dependency` or continue using `pedagogical_prerequisite` with disjoint `domain[]` arrays per the manifest's existing convention. Either is a legitimate path; the cross-bridge session adjudicates with full graph context.
- **Confidence-level composition skews INTERPRETED/SYNTHETIC.** Per [ADR 0030](0030-confidence-level-evidentiary-mode-on-nodes.md) (`confidence_level`: EXTRACTED | INTERPRETED | SYNTHETIC) and [ADR 0046](0046-structural-reference-posture-extends-to-philosophy-reference-works.md) (structural references provide priors for generative authoring, not extracted prose), philosophy nodes will be SYNTHETIC and INTERPRETED far more than EXTRACTED. Per-subdomain expectation in [`engine/build_readiness/phase_5.md`](../../engine/build_readiness/phase_5.md): `INTERPRETED ≥ 70%` floor, `SYNTHETIC ≤ 20%` ceiling, `EXTRACTED` the small remainder.
- **Cross-domain ratio threshold relaxed for philosophy.** Philosophy is more cross-domain-bridged than narrow STEM (epistemology bridges to philosophy of science, logic, philosophy of language; metaphysics bridges to philosophy of mind, ethics, philosophy of religion-when-readmitted; aesthetics bridges to ethics and philosophy of mind). The validator's `suspicious_cross_domain_ratio` soft-warn at 40% is appropriate for narrow domains but generates noise in philosophy. The phase_5.md gate report relaxes the per-subdomain threshold to 60% for the 9 subjects; the validator change ships in a routine-mode session that surfaces it.
- **Out-of-scope deferrals are durable, not silent.** Philosophy of religion and history-of-philosophy-as-subdomain are explicitly out of Phase 5 scope per this ADR's "Out of scope, deferred" section, with admit-criteria documented in the class-boundary clause. A future session that surfaces the deferral does not need to re-derive the reasoning; it inherits this ADR.
- **MemPalace decision drawer captured.** Per [ADR 0036](../../engine/adr/0036-expression-contract-for-inward-documents.md)'s two-layer rule: this ADR is the contract; the MemPalace `decision`-tagged drawer captures the conversational reasoning that produced the 9-subdomain split (the user's pushback on the canonical and STATE.md lists, the four adjudication criteria, the philosophy-of-religion deferral rationale, the class-boundary clause). Drawer authored in S-0045 alongside this ADR.
- **No supersession.** This ADR does not supersede any existing ADR. [ADR 0007](0007-cross-domain-porosity.md), [ADR 0008](0008-concept-nodes-not-thinkers.md), [ADR 0011](0011-no-hosted-copyrighted-material.md), [ADR 0030](0030-confidence-level-evidentiary-mode-on-nodes.md), [ADR 0046](0046-structural-reference-posture-extends-to-philosophy-reference-works.md) are all preserved and inherited.
