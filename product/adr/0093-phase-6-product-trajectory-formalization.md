# ADR 0093 — Phase 6 product-trajectory formalization: learner-facing OSS+BYOK, no LMS bundling, web visualizer BYOK deferred

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-05-18
- **Deciders:** S-0202 (PDG papers extraction pre-phase Session δ₁; sibling ADR 0094 lands the Phase 6 scope choice in the same session)

## Context

Phase 5 closed at S-0122 (production-audit closeout) + S-0123 (follow-up migrations); the seed graph carries 380 nodes / 533 edges. The PDG papers extraction pre-phase per [`HANDOFF.md`](../../HANDOFF.md) opened a six-session deliberation arc (Sessions α/β/γ/δ/ε/ζ) before any Phase 6 PDG implementation work. Sessions α (S-0199 — cross-reference audit at [`engine/build_readiness/pdg_papers_extraction/adr_cross_reference_map.md`](../../engine/build_readiness/pdg_papers_extraction/adr_cross_reference_map.md)), β (S-0200 — Kant/phenomenology walkthrough at [`kant_walkthrough.md`](../../engine/build_readiness/pdg_papers_extraction/kant_walkthrough.md)), and γ (S-0201 — foundational reading at [`foundations.md`](../../engine/build_readiness/pdg_papers_extraction/foundations.md)) are complete. This ADR plus sibling ADR 0094 (Phase 6 Scope) are the first two of Session δ's four foundational ADRs; the remaining two (tool-stack + learning-outcome taxonomy) plus the three Session-α coordination questions and the eight `kant_walkthrough.md` §6.7 D1-D8 schema items defer to Session δ₂+ per the user-approved S-0202 plan split.

[ADR 0065](0065-oss-pivot-and-byok-disposition.md) landed the OSS+BYOK disposition at S-0128: Paideia is an open-source project under Apache 2.0; users supply their own Anthropic API key on-device via iOS Keychain; institutional licensing, grants, and acquisition remain foreclosed. The eight commitments in ADR 0065 are the contract. What ADR 0065 deliberately did not enumerate — and what now matters for Phase 6 substrate decisions — is the **per-cluster product-trajectory implications** that the pre-phase Sessions α/β/γ surfaced. Cluster C15 (privacy/FERPA/NIST + override logging) and C17 (anomalous routing detection) carry institutional-flavored operational structures (cohort analytics, IRB review, instructor dashboards). Cluster C9 (instructional-spine five-service architecture) names "LLM orchestration" as a service without specifying client-side vs server-side under BYOK. The PDG papers' authoring assumes a richer institutional substrate than ADR 0065 commits Paideia to.

The user-adjudicated direction during the 2026-05-13/14 PDG papers extraction session (recorded in `HANDOFF.md` "Decisions already settled in this session" — decision bullet 1, and in engine_memory drawer [`426a6230`](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia)): *Paideia product trajectory — learner-facing + open-source/forkable; **NO LMS-integrated tooling on projects the user is directly involved with** (third-party LMS integrations of the OSS graph are not foreclosed)*. The trajectory bullet was settled in conversation but never ADR'd; Phase 6 Scope ADR 0094 needs to cite a binding trajectory contract rather than a HANDOFF prose paragraph. This ADR formalizes the trajectory bullet — including its derivative implications for Phase 6 cluster scope — so ADR 0094 can rest on a cited ADR rather than reach behind to a `HANDOFF.md` paragraph.

Three sub-questions Phase 6 entry needs settled, or explicitly deferred:

- **LMS-integration posture for Paideia's own releases.** Does the Paideia maintainer ship LMS-bundled releases (Blackboard/Canvas/Moodle integrations) under the OSS pivot? The 2026-05-14 direction says no. This ADR locks it as a binding commitment.
- **Web visualizer BYOK shape.** ADR 0065 commitment 1 names "public knowledge-graph visualizer demonstrating prerequisite topology" as *operational, not product*. ADR 0065 commitment 8 commits the iOS app's BYOK shape (Keychain client-side, no Paideia proxy). The web visualizer's BYOK shape is structurally distinct: a web client cannot use iOS Keychain. Is the web visualizer (i) BYOK against a browser-stored key the user pastes per-session, (ii) read-only graph display with no LLM calls (no BYOK surface), or (iii) some hybrid? This ADR defers the choice to the Phase 8/9 implementation session that builds the visualizer, because the choice depends on what the visualizer is for (Phase 6 substrate work does not need this settled).
- **Retention-mechanic exclusion scope under Phase 6 feature work.** ADR 0065 commitment 6 (no social/sharing/leaderboards/streaks/retention-nudges) is locked. Phase 6 substrate ADRs may add features tangent to retention (e.g., alternative-entry-route UI per C11 workflow #1; mastery-snapshot export per ADR 0031). Does commitment 6 forbid alternative-entry-route navigation? This ADR confirms the discipline: **pedagogically-defensible navigation is permitted; retention-mechanic features are not**, and the distinction is the same one ADR 0065 commitment 6 already draws (the threat shape is engagement-time-as-optimization-target, not pedagogical-utility).

## Decision

The Paideia Phase 6 product trajectory is four binding commitments, each derived from ADR 0065's eight commitments but specialized for the Phase 6 substrate-work surface:

**(1) NO LMS-integrated tooling in Paideia's own releases.** The Paideia maintainer ships only the learner-direct artifacts ADR 0065 commitment 1 names: the App Store iOS binary, the OSS source code, and operational web surfaces (marketing site, public knowledge-graph visualizer demonstrating prerequisite topology, OSS community demo pages). The maintainer does not ship Blackboard / Canvas / Moodle / Brightspace integrations, does not maintain LTI provider/consumer endpoints, does not author LMS-side gradebook adapters. **Third-party forks of the OSS graph and engine apparatus that integrate with LMS systems are not foreclosed by the OSS license** — Apache 2.0 permits such forks, and the OSS pivot's artifact-survival rationale (ADR 0065 Context paragraph 2) explicitly admits "anyone who finds the pedagogical contract worth carrying" can fork and operate the artifact. This commitment governs the maintainer's release scope, not the license's permissive shape. The institutional-foreclosure half of ADR 0065 commitment 4 already forecloses institutional licensing as a vendor offering; this commitment extends that foreclosure to LMS integrations on the same logic — the maintainer does not pursue institutional surfaces, of which LMS bundling is one.

**(2) Individual-only product scope is re-affirmed for Phase 6 substrate work.** ADR 0065 commitment 4 forecloses institutional licensing, grants, and acquisition. Phase 6 substrate ADRs (cluster-implementation ADRs in Session δ₂+ and subsequent build sessions) inherit the foreclosure: schema additions cannot presuppose institutional governance structures (cohort analytics, IRB workflows, instructor dashboards) as load-bearing. Where pre-phase synthesis material (Clusters C15/C16/C17) frames features in institutional-governance language, the Phase 6 ADRs adopting those clusters must either (a) re-scope to consumer-individual deployment, (b) explicitly defer the institutional aspects to a Phase 7+ "research deployment" track Paideia does not ship, or (c) decline the institutional framing entirely. The choice is per-cluster and per-ADR; the binding commitment here is that institutional-as-Paideia-product is foreclosed for Phase 6.

**(3) BYOK execution surface for Phase 6 is iOS Keychain client-side; the web visualizer's BYOK shape is explicitly deferred to the Phase 8/9 implementation session.** ADR 0065 commitment 8 commits the iOS app to on-device Keychain storage with `kSecAttrAccessibleWhenUnlockedThisDeviceOnly` and a server that never sees the key, never proxies the API, never observes the API exchange. Phase 6 substrate work inherits this: cluster-implementation ADRs that touch LLM orchestration (per Cluster C9, C10, C11, C14, C15) must commit to client-side LLM-call architecture for the iOS app. **The web visualizer's BYOK shape — browser-stored key vs no-LLM-calls vs hybrid — is deferred to the Phase 8/9 implementation session that builds the visualizer.** The visualizer is "operational, not product" per ADR 0065 commitment 1, and Phase 6 substrate decisions do not depend on this choice. Session δ₂'s Tool-stack ADR may comment on the visualizer's storage-tier requirements but does not pre-commit the visualizer's LLM-call architecture.

**(4) Retention-mechanic exclusion holds for Phase 6 feature scope; pedagogically-defensible navigation is permitted.** ADR 0065 commitment 6 forbids social / sharing / leaderboards / streaks / retention-nudges / community surface / in-app messaging. Phase 6 substrate work may add navigation primitives (alternative-entry-route UI per the pre-phase Cluster C11 workflow #1; mastery-snapshot export per ADR 0031; comparative-lens cross-reference UI per Cluster C4) that serve pedagogical pluralism without being retention mechanics. **The distinction is the threat-shape test from ADR 0065 commitment 6:** does the feature treat engagement-time-as-optimization-target (retention mechanic, forbidden), or does it serve learner pedagogical agency without engagement-time framing (navigation primitive, permitted)? Phase 6 ADRs adopting feature work must apply this test explicitly in their Decision section.

## Alternatives Considered

### Defer the Product Trajectory ADR entirely; inline trajectory rationale in Phase 6 Scope ADR

- **What:** Skip authoring ADR 0093 in S-0202; have sibling ADR 0094 (Phase 6 Scope) inline the trajectory rationale from `HANDOFF.md` "Decisions already settled in this session" directly into its Context section.
- **Pros:** One fewer ADR to author; tighter Session δ₁ scope; the trajectory rationale is short enough to inline (~3 paragraphs).
- **Cons:** ADR 0094's load-bearing premises rest on the trajectory commitments (especially the foreclosure of Option C "halt PDG work"); a future session reading ADR 0094 cold would need to chase the trajectory through `HANDOFF.md` prose plus the 2026-05-14 engine_memory drawer rather than a citable ADR. Future cluster-implementation ADRs (Session δ₂+, Phase 6 implementation sessions) need to cite the trajectory; an ADR is the citation target; a `HANDOFF.md` paragraph plus a memory drawer is not.
- **Rejected because:** the two-layer decision-recording discipline per [CLAUDE.md](../../CLAUDE.md) "Standing rules" treats ADRs as the contract layer; settled product-trajectory commitments belong in ADRs, not in `HANDOFF.md` prose. The cost of authoring this ADR is bounded (~30-60 min per the S-0202 plan); the cost of not authoring it propagates across every Phase 6 implementation ADR that would otherwise need to inline-cite a prose paragraph.

### Re-litigate ADR 0065 commitments under the PDG papers extraction lens

- **What:** Treat the pre-phase Sessions α/β/γ findings as grounds for reopening ADR 0065 commitments — e.g., the synthesis paper's Cluster C16 (learner-outcome evaluation infrastructure) names a three-arm comparison study as the canonical Phase 7+ first deployment; that's research-shaped work; should ADR 0065 commitment 4's institutional-foreclosure reopen to admit research deployments?
- **Pros:** Aligns Paideia's product surface with the pre-phase corpus's pedagogically-richer institutional shape. Captures research-infrastructure value the OSS forkable-by-anyone framing does not directly enable.
- **Cons:** Out of scope for Session δ₁. ADR 0065's refusal-not-deferral discipline binds — reopening commitment 4 requires explicit supersession with named context for why the disposition changed; the pre-phase synthesis findings are insufficient ground for that supersession (the synthesis paper's authoring assumed an institutional substrate the project does not commit to, but the project's commitment is what binds, not the synthesis's assumption). Conflates "the literature assumes X" with "the project should commit to X."
- **Rejected because:** ADR 0065's `Status: Accepted` is binding; supersessions require explicit reasoning per the supersession discipline in [`engine/operations/adr-authoring.md`](../../engine/operations/adr-authoring.md); the pre-phase corpus does not supply that reasoning. The right disposition for institutional-shaped synthesis content is per-cluster re-scoping (commitment 2 above), not ADR 0065 supersession.

### Commit the web visualizer BYOK shape now (rather than deferring)

- **What:** Use Session δ₁ to settle whether the public knowledge-graph visualizer per ADR 0065 commitment 1 is (i) BYOK against a browser-stored key, (ii) read-only graph display with no LLM calls, or (iii) some hybrid.
- **Pros:** Removes one open item from the trajectory; gives Phase 8/9 implementation a clearer target.
- **Cons:** The choice depends on what the visualizer is for — pure topology demonstration (no LLM), or interactive-graph-with-explanation (LLM-mediated, BYOK required). That depends on Phase 8/9 implementation context Session δ₁ does not have. Pre-committing without that context would force ADR 0093 to either name the visualizer scope prematurely or carry an unverified premise.
- **Rejected because:** the deferral is bounded — Phase 6 substrate work does not depend on the web visualizer's BYOK shape; the Phase 8/9 implementation session has the context to choose. Pre-committing introduces an unverified premise into Session δ₁'s scope without benefit to Phase 6 substrate decisions.

*(ADR 0084's extraction step is not triggered for this ADR. The four commitments are formalizations of already-settled premises in ADR 0065 + the 2026-05-14 PDG papers extraction session direction. No novel load-bearing premise is introduced that the author has not already tested. The web visualizer BYOK shape — the one sub-question this ADR encounters that could carry an unverified premise — is explicitly deferred rather than committed, sidestepping the premise.)*

## Consequences

- **Phase 6 substrate work proceeds with cited trajectory clarity.** Sibling ADR 0094 (Phase 6 Scope) cites this ADR's commitments 1-4 in its Context section. Future cluster-implementation ADRs in Session δ₂+ and subsequent build sessions cite this ADR when their Decision sections need to invoke (a) the LMS-foreclosure, (b) the individual-only scope, (c) the BYOK execution surface, or (d) the retention-mechanic exclusion threat-shape test.

- **HANDOFF.md "Decisions already settled in this session" updates in this session.** The bullet labeled "Paideia product trajectory — learner-facing + open-source/forkable; **NO LMS-integrated tooling on projects user is directly involved with** (third-party LMS integrations of the OSS graph not foreclosed)" is replaced with a pointer to this ADR. The decision is now ADR'd; the HANDOFF prose is retired-by-reference. The other four "Decisions already settled" bullets (two-bias-surface partition; contestability is atomic; mass-retyping default; quality-first deliberation posture) remain in HANDOFF prose pending their own ADRs in Session δ₂+ or later.

- **Web visualizer BYOK shape is named as a Phase 8/9 implementation decision.** The Phase 8/9 implementation session that builds the visualizer carries the decision; no Phase 6 substrate ADR should pre-commit. The deferral is explicit, not silent — future sessions reading commitment 3 see the deferral surface and know not to invent the answer.

- **No retroactive migrations.** This ADR is formalization of already-settled commitments; no schema changes, no code changes, no per-cluster migrations.

- **No first-exercise readiness note per [ADR 0053](../../engine/adr/0053-mechanism-first-exercise-gate.md).** This is not a cross-cutting mechanism; it is a product-trajectory formalization. ADR 0053's trigger criteria #1-#4 do not fire (no new wrapper around a harness surface; no novel cross-session protocol; no first-exercise gate semantics).

- **No back-pointer cascade to ADR 0065.** ADR 0065 is not superseded by this ADR; this ADR specializes ADR 0065's commitments for the Phase 6 substrate-work surface. ADR 0065's `Status: Accepted` is unchanged. Per [ADR 0041](../../engine/adr/0041-cascade-discipline-three-validator-checks-plus-manual-procedures.md), back-pointer cascade is for supersession and back-reference orphan classes; this ADR is neither.

- **The two-layer decision recording is satisfied by this ADR + the matching engine_memory `decisions`-room drawer authored in S-0202.** The drawer captures the conversational deliberation that settled the ADR shape (the user-approved scope split, the three sub-question framing, the rejected alternatives' reasoning). The PostToolUse hook on ADR writes (`engine/tools/hooks/post-adr-write.sh` per [ADR 0043](../../engine/adr/0043-hook-architecture.md)) will soft-warn if the drawer is missing; expectation is that it surfaces silently.
