# ADR 0094 — Phase 6 scope: expand to include Tier-A substrate redesign (Clusters 1-5) before SEP/embedding self-correction work

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-05-18
- **Deciders:** S-0202 (PDG papers extraction pre-phase Session δ₁; sibling ADR 0093 lands the product-trajectory formalization in the same session)

## Context

Phase 6 was unblocked at S-0152 via the OQ-DEC1-A/B/C/D tension-set settlement ([ADR 0085](0085-server-side-mastery-computation-confirmed.md) server-side mastery; [ADR 0086](0086-model-agnostic-embedding-storage-architecture.md) per-dim embedding partitions; [ADR 0087](0087-two-hop-neighborhood-retrieval-shape.md) two-hop retrieval; [ADR 0088](0088-sep-chunk-resolver-index.md) SEP chunk-resolver index). The unblocked Phase 6 self-correction master plan covers — per STATE.md "Current phase" and [`engine/build_readiness/pdg_papers_extraction/synthesis.md`](../../engine/build_readiness/pdg_papers_extraction/synthesis.md) §"Phase 6 master-plan-input subsection" — `users.embedding_model` metadata, vector partition migration, the `sep_chunks` junction table, 380-node SEP backfill, entity-resolution-service application code, and tension-log emission scaffolding. The work is ready to execute against the existing nodes table.

The PDG papers extraction pre-phase (Sessions α/β/γ at S-0199/S-0200/S-0201; this ADR's session is Session δ₁) surfaced a 17-cluster synthesis at [`synthesis.md`](../../engine/build_readiness/pdg_papers_extraction/synthesis.md) that partitions PDG work into five tiers:

- **Tier A — Substrate redesign** (Clusters 1-5): contestability per-edge (C1); edge-type taxonomy expansion + three-relation layered separation (C2); goal-relative parameterization (C3); node schema redesign (C4); misconception sub-graph encoding (C5). Dependency order: 1 → 2 → 4 → 3 → 5. The synthesis frames this tier as the substrate redesign the current Paideia graph (380 nodes, 533 edges, 2 edge types) is materially under-modeled against.
- **Tier B — Authoring discipline** (Clusters 6-7): backward-design + Decoding-the-Disciplines (C6); graph predictive-validity extension to SQA discipline (C7).
- **Tier C — Existing-anti-bias-mechanism extension** (Cluster 8): graph-topology bias mitigation.
- **Tier D — Phase 7+ teaching architecture** (Clusters 9-13): instructional-spine, prompt template + content store, four workflows, scaffolding fade, alternative-entry-route.
- **Tier E — Phase 7+ deployment governance** (Clusters 14-17): LLM-mediated output-side bias, privacy/FERPA/NIST + override logging, learner-outcome evaluation infrastructure, anomalous routing detection.

[`HANDOFF.md`](../../HANDOFF.md) PDG entry framed the Phase 6 scope decision as four options — A (expand to all 17 clusters across full philosophy subdomains); B (narrow to Tier-A substrate subset C1-C5); C (halt PDG work pending institutional/grant clarity); D (rescope to a different organizing angle). After [ADR 0093](0093-phase-6-product-trajectory-formalization.md) commitment (b) (individual-only scope re-affirmed; institutional foreclosed) the four-option framing reduces: **Option C is structurally foreclosed** (ADR 0093 commitment 2 inherits ADR 0065 commitment 4's refusal-not-deferral binding); **Option D is underspecified** (HANDOFF prose does not name a concrete alternative angle); **Option A is too large** (17 clusters span substrate + pedagogy + LLM + governance + evaluation; far exceeds one phase). The viable adjudication is between something Option-B-shaped (expand Phase 6 to include Tier-A substrate) and the status quo (proceed with the S-0152-unblocked self-correction master plan; defer Tier-A).

[`synthesis.md`](../../engine/build_readiness/pdg_papers_extraction/synthesis.md) §"Phase 6 master-plan-input subsection" reframes the choice more empirically as a binary, accounting for the existing self-correction master plan:

- **Synthesis Option α — Expand Phase 6 scope to include Tier-A substrate (Clusters 1, 2, 4 at minimum) before SEP backfill.** Adds ~4-6 session-sized work units. SEP backfill operates on the redesigned nodes table — embeddings are computed against `node_type` / `disciplinary_domain` / `granularity` / `canonical_sources` / `misconceptions` (per [`adversarial_review.md`](../../engine/build_readiness/pdg_papers_extraction/adversarial_review.md) E.12.2, these five fields most affect embedding semantic quality if absent at backfill time). Architecturally coherent; higher upfront cost; avoids re-backfill.
- **Synthesis Option β — Proceed narrow; accept a follow-up re-backfill.** Tier-A lands after Phase 6 SEP work. Existing nodes are embedded based on current sparse representations. After Tier-A migrations add the five semantically-rich fields, a follow-up re-backfill recomputes embeddings. Lower upfront cost; SEP work proceeds on the existing schema; re-backfill cost is the cost-of-choice.

The HANDOFF four-option framing was the planning shorthand; the synthesis-derived binary is the actual choice the session adjudicates.

Three Session-α coordination questions (per `adr_cross_reference_map.md` cross-cluster integration sections) plus eight D1-D8 schema items (per `kant_walkthrough.md` §6.7) plus the two foundational ADRs that defer to Session δ₂+ (tool-stack ADR; learning-outcome taxonomy ADR) are upstream substrate-shape inputs the Tier-A migrations cite. The Phase 6 scope decision is independent of those settlements in the following sense: the scope ADR commits to *which clusters land in Phase 6*; the cluster-implementation ADRs commit to *what shape each cluster's migration takes*. The latter rest on the upstream settlements; the former does not.

[ADR 0084](../../engine/adr/0084-pushback-rule-extraction-step-for-high-stakes-decisions.md) applies — this is a **contract-shape change** under the ADR 0084 trigger classes (Phase 6 scope determines which schema commitments propagate to Phase 6 cluster-implementation ADRs, and the Tier-A migrations rewrite load-bearing nodes-table and edges-table contracts). The Load-bearing premises subsection below dogfoods the extraction step.

## Decision

**Phase 6 expands to include Tier-A substrate redesign (Clusters 1-5) before the SEP/embedding self-correction work unblocked by ADRs 0085-0088 ships its node-table-dependent migrations.** Per synthesis.md §"Phase 6 master-plan-input subsection" this is Synthesis Option α (the expand option); per `HANDOFF.md` PDG entry's four-option framing this is Option B (narrow to Tier-A substrate subset). Concrete cluster set in Phase 6:

- **Cluster 1 — Contestability substrate** (per-edge confidence + provenance + counterexamples + version history; one product ADR + one migration).
- **Cluster 2 — Edge-type taxonomy expansion + three-relation layered separation** (Layer 1 pedagogical / Layer 2 historical / Layer 3 conceptual; one product ADR + one migration).
- **Cluster 4 — Node schema redesign** (`node_type` + `disciplinary_domain` + `granularity` + `canonical_sources` + `misconceptions` + audience/equity metadata; one product ADR + one migration; depends on coordination question #1 and D1-D5 + D7-D8 settlements from Session δ₂+).
- **Cluster 3 — Goal-relative parameterization** (`learning_outcomes` table + edge-outcome necessity field; one product ADR + one migration; depends on the learning-outcome taxonomy ADR from Session δ₂).
- **Cluster 5 — Misconception sub-graph encoding** (extends C2's edge layers + C4's `misconceptions` field; one product ADR + one migration; depends on D6 + D7 settlements from Session δ₂+).

Dependency order: **1 → 2 → 4 → 3 → 5**. SEP backfill + embedding work (per ADR 0086 / ADR 0088) operates on the Tier-A-redesigned schema, so the five semantically-rich fields per `adversarial_review.md` E.12.2 (`node_type` / `disciplinary_domain` / `granularity` / `canonical_sources` / `misconceptions`) are present at backfill time. The unblocked Phase 6 self-correction master plan's other work (entity-resolution-service application code; tension-log emission scaffolding; vector partition migration; `users.embedding_model` metadata; `sep_chunks` junction table per ADR 0088) is unchanged in shape — these surfaces do not depend on Tier-A redesigned columns.

**Tier B/C/D/E (Clusters 6-17) defer to Phase 7+ or are out-of-scope for the maintainer's release path** per ADR 0093 commitment 1 (no LMS bundling) and commitment 2 (institutional foreclosed). Specifically:

- **Tier B (Clusters 6-7)** — actionable in parallel per the synthesis's tier framing, but defer to Phase 6.5 or Phase 7+; C6 (Decoding-the-Disciplines authoring discipline) is an operational document, not schema; C7 (graph predictive-validity extension to SQA) extends an existing Paideia operational discipline and can land outside the Phase 6 substrate ADR cycle.
- **Tier C (Cluster 8)** — graph-topology bias mitigation defers to Phase 7+; depends on Tier-A's `tradition_label` (C4) and on the alternative-entry-route concept (Tier D's C13). Phase 6 prepares the substrate; Phase 7+ wires the mechanism.
- **Tier D (Clusters 9-13)** — Phase 7+ teaching architecture; explicit deferral. ADR 0093 commitment 1 (no LMS bundling) and commitment 3 (BYOK execution surface — iOS Keychain client-side; web visualizer BYOK deferred) constrain the shape these clusters take when Phase 7+ opens; this ADR does not commit Phase 7+ content beyond noting the deferral.
- **Tier E (Clusters 14-17)** — Phase 7+ deployment governance; explicit deferral. Per ADR 0093 commitment 2, the institutional-flavored framings of C15 (privacy/FERPA/NIST), C16 (learner-outcome evaluation infrastructure), and C17 (anomalous routing detection) must be per-cluster re-scoped to consumer-individual deployment OR explicitly deferred to a Phase 7+ "research deployment" track Paideia does not ship.

Session δ₂+ adjudicates the three Session-α coordination questions and the eight `kant_walkthrough.md` §6.7 D1-D8 schema items before the Phase 6 cluster-implementation ADRs land. The settlements are inputs to the Cluster 4 and Cluster 5 ADR shapes specifically; this ADR (Phase 6 Scope) is independent of them.

## Alternatives Considered

### Synthesis Option α — Expand Phase 6 scope to include Tier-A substrate before SEP backfill *(chosen)*

- **What:** Land Clusters 1-5 migrations before the SEP/embedding self-correction work commits to the existing nodes-table. SEP backfill operates on the redesigned schema; embeddings are computed against the five Tier-A semantically-rich fields.
- **Pros:** Architecturally coherent. Avoids the re-backfill cost (synthesis's named cost of Option β). Per `adversarial_review.md` E.12.2, the five Tier-A fields most affect embedding semantic quality; embedding against them is materially better than against current sparse representations. Aligns with quality-first deliberation posture per the user-pushback drawer 1dc03200. Phase 6 self-correction master plan's other work (entity-resolution, tension-log, vector partitions, users.embedding_model, sep_chunks) is unchanged in shape — only the timing of the node-table-dependent migrations shifts to land after Tier-A.
- **Cons:** Adds ~4-6 session-sized work units to Phase 6. Cluster 4 and Cluster 5 cluster-implementation ADRs depend on Session δ₂+ adjudication of D-items and coordination questions; Phase 6 substrate ADRs cannot all land in parallel — substantive serialization is required. Premise 2 (Tier-A is implementable without re-decomposition) and premise 3 (D-item adjudication doesn't force Tier-D inclusion) are unverifiable in-context.
- **Rejected because:** not rejected — chosen.

### Synthesis Option β — Proceed narrow; accept a follow-up re-backfill

- **What:** Phase 6 ships the S-0152-unblocked self-correction master plan against the current nodes table. Tier-A substrate migrations land after Phase 6 in a separate phase or via per-Issue prioritization. Once Tier-A redesigns the nodes table, a follow-up re-backfill recomputes embeddings against the Tier-A-enriched fields.
- **Pros:** Lower upfront cost — Phase 6 ships sooner. SEP work proceeds on the schema the project already has; no schema-evolution coordination across substrate-and-self-correction migration sequencing. Bounded scope per the existing master plan.
- **Cons:** Re-backfill cost (embedding recomputation across 380 nodes after schema enrichment) is the synthesis's named cost. Per `adversarial_review.md` E.12.2 the five Tier-A fields materially affect embedding semantic quality; embedding against sparse current representations may produce results that need substantial re-work. The S-0152-unblocked self-correction's downstream consumers (entity-resolution, two-hop retrieval per ADR 0087) operate on embeddings; if the first-pass embeddings underperform, the self-correction work's value is degraded until re-backfill lands. Violates quality-first deliberation posture per pushback drawer 1dc03200 ("set the stage fully before stepping into the next stage" applies here as much as it does to the pre-phase deliberation).
- **Rejected because:** the embedding semantic-quality argument (premise 6) is concrete: the five Tier-A fields are named in `adversarial_review.md` E.12.2 as the most-affecting set; re-backfill cost is a real cost the synthesis identifies; quality-first posture favors landing the substrate before the work that depends on the substrate. The synthesis's adversarial finding is the corpus's strongest claim on this question; choosing Option β would override that claim without empirical counter-evidence.

### HANDOFF Option A — Expand Phase 6 to include all 17 synthesis clusters across full philosophy subdomains

- **What:** Land cluster-implementation ADRs and migrations for C1-C17 in Phase 6. Tier-A substrate + Tier-B authoring discipline + Tier-C bias mitigation + Tier-D teaching architecture + Tier-E deployment governance all ship.
- **Pros:** Comprehensive; aligns Phase 6's surface with the full synthesis-paper recommendation. Future Phase 7+ work has no remaining substrate gaps.
- **Cons:** 17 clusters span substrate + pedagogy + LLM orchestration + governance + evaluation. Each cluster is 1-2 session-sized work units minimum; the joint set far exceeds one phase's reasonable scope (synthesis's own framing notes that "Tier A is most consequential and should land in dependency order... Tiers B + C are immediately actionable in parallel. Tiers D + E are Phase 7+"). Tier D + E clusters depend on architectural decisions Paideia has not yet made (instructional-spine architecture per C9; LMS integration scope per C15-C17). Tier E's institutional-flavored framings (C15-C17) require per-cluster re-scoping that itself is substantive Session δ₂+ work.
- **Rejected because:** the cluster count exceeds reasonable phase scope by a factor of 3-4×; the synthesis itself does not propose this as a viable option in its master-plan-input subsection; ADR 0093 commitment 2 forecloses the institutional framings Tier E carries, requiring substantial per-cluster re-scoping work before those clusters even become drafting-ready.

### HANDOFF Option C — Halt PDG work pending institutional/grant clarity

- **What:** Do not pursue PDG-driven substrate changes in Phase 6. Continue Phase 6 self-correction master plan as currently scoped (no Tier-A). Re-evaluate PDG scope in a future phase once institutional/grant pathways open.
- **Pros:** Preserves optionality for institutional pivot. Lowest immediate cost.
- **Cons:** ADR 0065 commitment 4 + ADR 0093 commitment (b) foreclose institutional licensing, grants, and acquisition as exit by *refusal, not deferral*. Halting PDG work pending an institutional pathway that the project has committed will not open is a deferred-loss disposition — the work either lands or does not, and "wait for institutional clarity" maps to "never land" under the existing trajectory commitments.
- **Rejected because:** structurally foreclosed by [ADR 0065](0065-oss-pivot-and-byok-disposition.md) commitment 4 + [ADR 0093](0093-phase-6-product-trajectory-formalization.md) commitment (b). Reopening would require explicit supersession of both ADRs with named context for why the institutional foreclosure changed; this session does not perform that supersession.

### HANDOFF Option D — Rescope to a different organizing angle

- **What:** Reframe Phase 6 around a different organizing principle than synthesis cluster-driven substrate work. Candidate angles from the corpus: "focus on SQA validation of existing edges rather than schema expansion" (shifts Phase 6 from structure-building to quality-testing); or "focus on LLM-integration contract rather than graph substrate" (defers substrate work and jumps to teaching-layer ADRs).
- **Pros:** Concrete alternative angles are pre-named in the pre-phase corpus; not all are equally foreclosed.
- **Cons:** Both named angles defer substrate work — the SQA-validation angle requires existing-edges to remain stable (which Tier-A substrate redesign breaks); the LLM-integration angle is downstream of substrate per the synthesis's dependency ordering (Tier-D depends on Tier-A). Neither escapes the substrate-work-before-downstream-work logic; they reorder rather than avoid. The S-0152-unblocked Phase 6 self-correction master plan would still need to ship in some form; this option's gain is unclear.
- **Rejected because:** Option D as named in `HANDOFF.md` is underspecified — no concrete alternative angle is proposed with sufficient grounding to adjudicate against Synthesis Options α/β. If a future session surfaces a specific Option-D shape with concrete benefits, ADR 0094 can be superseded; absent that, the binary between Synthesis Options α and β is the actual adjudication and Option α is chosen on the grounds above.
