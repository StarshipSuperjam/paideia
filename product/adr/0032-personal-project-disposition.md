## Context

[ADR 0002](0002-commercial-sustainability-without-pedagogical-compromise.md) framed Paideia's economic shape as commercial sustainability with pedagogical integrity as the hard constraint on revenue mechanisms. The framing presumed an open option set: subscriptions, institutional licensing, premium content surfaces, paid expert review queues, eventual non-profit incorporation, eventual grant funding, eventual Khan Academy acquisition. The pedagogical-integrity guard was load-bearing because the option set was open — once revenue logic touches what gets taught or how, the teaching system optimizes for whatever the revenue function rewards. ADR 0002 named the failure mode and erected the discipline against it.

The discipline is sound. The premise — that the option set should remain open — turned out to be the corruption vector.

A pattern surfaced during the S-0008–S-0011 exploration arc: every "leave the option open to expand later" decision pulled the design toward business outcomes that pedagogy would never have warranted on its own. The community-college-audience-vs-market split (`docs/business.md` Audience vs. Market: Community College) was an honest read of which institutions could plausibly buy the product, which made it a *commercial* read whose only pedagogical content was the freshman-defaults calibration — and the freshman calibration is justified by the asymmetry of failure (per [ADR 0012](0012-freshman-defaults-autodidact-ceiling.md)), not by the audience-as-market framing. Khan Academy as acquisition exit (`docs/business.md` Acquisition as Exit Path) added zero pedagogical value and a non-trivial design pull (build for a buyer's eye, not a learner's). The institutional schema provisions in `docs/architecture.md` (`cohort_id`, shareable constrained paths) reserved capacity for an institutional regime that would never ship if the project's actual disposition were honest. Syllabus upload's institutional-wedge framing (`docs/business.md` Syllabus Upload as Institutional Wedge) treated a personal-use feature as a sales lever. Non-profit incorporation (`docs/business.md` Non-Profit Incorporation) presumed grant funding which presumed institutional partners which presumed the regime the project no longer wants. BYOK regime work (OQ-BYOK-REGIME, [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md)) leaned institutional specifically because it sidestepped the cost-ceiling builder-exposure problem — but that lean only mattered if the institutional regime were on the table.

Each of these decisions was individually defensible. Cumulatively, they were a slow drift toward optimizing for revenue mechanisms the builder no longer wants the project to depend on. ADR 0002's "revenue logic must never change *what or how* the product teaches" worked — what it did not constrain was whether the product *should be designed at all* against revenue mechanisms whose existence creates funnel pressure (retention metrics, conversion ceremonies, free-tier-as-acquisition-channel framing). The failure mode is no longer revenue-logic-touching-pedagogy; it is *builder*-funnel-mechanic-touching-pedagogy. Building for a market — even a market the builder believes deserves the product — pulls the builder's attention toward retention and conversion in ways pedagogy would not warrant.

The fix is not a stricter version of ADR 0002. The fix is foreclosing the optionality whose preservation drove the drift.

The S-0011 conversation settled the disposition. The supersession ADR is the artifact recording it. [ADR 0031](0031-erasure-mechanism-and-individual-only-regime.md) was authored ahead of this ADR because its simplifications (hard-delete-with-cascade settles cleanly without an institutional contractual regime to design audit-trail preservation for; OQ-PRIVACY-B is moot under individual-only data) depended on the disposition being settled, and the privacy ADRs were the active Phase 1.4 work. ADR 0031 named the dependency in its Consequences and committed the supersession to S-0012 — which is this ADR.

## Decision

**Paideia ships as a personal project the builder makes available to other people via a single iOS App Store cost-priced subscription. The option to grow into an institutional product, a non-profit, a grant-funded organization, or an acquisition target is foreclosed *as a refusal, not a deferral* — including in the success case.**

The disposition has six binding commitments.

**(1) Single-platform iOS via App Store.** No Android, no web app, no Linux/Mac desktop client. Distribution is the Apple App Store — not sideload, not TestFlight-only at steady state. Apple Developer Program enrollment ($99/yr) is the Phase 8 forcing function. The DeepTutor fork (Apache 2.0) is consulted only for what serves the iOS native target. The narrowing is structural: if a future session proposes Android or web, the proposal must supersede this ADR; the cost it pays is the supersession discipline.

**(2) Cost-priced subscription via Apple In-App Purchase. No free tier.** Target $12.99/mo monthly with an annual subscription option at the typical 15–20% discount. Apple's Small Business Program (15% take rate under $1M annual revenue) is presumed. The App Store screenshots and the 7-day Apple refund window function as the trial affordance — a separate free-tier UX is *not* engineered, because a free tier is the canonical builder-funnel-mechanic that pulls pedagogy toward retention and conversion ceremony. Pricing language remains learning-meaningful, not vendor-SKU-meaningful (per the principle preserved from `docs/business.md` Revenue Mechanisms — vendor model choices are internal optimization, not pricing surface). The single tier is structurally necessary for the no-funnel-mechanic discipline; tiering by learning intensity is foreclosed at this disposition's scale.

**(3) Builder eats API costs within a fixed annual operating subsidy budget. No grants, no 501(c)(3), no institutional licensing.** The builder's personal subsidy budget — order-of-magnitude $300/year for infrastructure (Apple Developer Program + VPS + domain), plus the marginal API cost of the builder's own use — covers the operating floor. Paid users at $12.99/mo cover their own marginal API at the price point's design margin. The subsidy budget is *not* expected to fund free-tier API consumption (no free tier exists) and is *not* expected to scale with user count. The numeric value of the budget remains private operational configuration consistent with [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md)'s discipline; the principle the value implements is now reframed (see commitment 5).

**(4) No bring-your-own-key, neither consumer nor institutional. No institutional regime, period.** OQ-BYOK-REGIME is closed by foreclosure in this ADR (do not author a separate ADR for OQ-BYOK-REGIME — its institutional candidate is excluded by the no-institutional-regime commitment, and its consumer candidate self-selects technically sophisticated users away from the freshman audience the system is calibrated for per [ADR 0012](0012-freshman-defaults-autodidact-ceiling.md)). The institutional schema provisions in `docs/architecture.md` (`cohort_id` field, shareable-constrained-paths-as-institutional-wedge framing) are dead-weight whose removal lands in this session alongside the docs rewrites. Syllabus upload may survive as a personal-use feature; its sales-wedge / institutional-onboarding framing does not.

**(5) [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md) is reframed as fixed annual operating subsidy budget — nothing ever raises it.** The cost-ceiling principle ADR 0029 commits is unchanged in mechanism (per-user cap, aggregate cap, real-time spend telemetry, defined cap-behavior, soft-walls-degrade-don't-terminate). What changes is the framing of the ceiling's value. ADR 0029's *Grant timeline interacts with this commitment* consequence — the bullet that contemplated a grant landing and the ceiling being raised — is retracted. There is no future grant. The ceiling is a fixed annual operating subsidy budget the builder commits in advance and does not exceed; if usage growth would push spend past the budget, the response is to raise prices to slow growth, cap signups, or pause the App Store listing — never to pursue institutional clients, grants, or acquisition. Specifically: **if this scales beyond what the personal subsidy budget supports, the response is to raise prices to slow growth or to cap signups, not to pursue institutional clients, grants, or acquisition.** The numeric ceiling value remains private operational configuration. The mechanism survives intact; only the value's *justification structure* changes (subsidy budget, not bridge-to-grants).

**(6) Polish is static, never dynamic. Maintenance is minimum-shape, deliberately.** The polish target is "as polished as solo + AI can manage" along static surfaces only — visual design, typography, animation, copy quality, layout, iconography, sound design, haptics. Dynamic-feature surfaces that would require the builder to do something on a recurring schedule for the app to feel alive are explicitly foreclosed: no social, no sharing, no leaderboards, no streaks, no push notifications beyond auth/billing, no community, no in-app messaging, no "what's new" surfaces. Maintenance posture is minimum-shape up front: no live support email surface (FAQ-only support); release cadence at builder discretion (4–8 week typical baseline); the builder is not a customer service department. The success criterion below is the load-bearing test for whether a feature belongs — *not* market data.

**Success criterion: "An app I would pay for if it weren't mine."** This is a self-applying criterion: the builder is the test, and the builder's bias toward "knowing how the system works" obscures whether the system is usable cold. The builder-bias failure mode is what keeps two existing ADRs load-bearing for new reasons: [ADR 0012](0012-freshman-defaults-autodidact-ceiling.md) (freshman defaults — calibration for a learner encountering ideas for the first time *cold*) and [ADR 0027](0027-rendering-policy-prompt-layer-contract.md) (rendering policy worked example — the gradeable test artifact at Phase 7) protect against builder-bias drift, not just against pedagogical drift or market-fit drift. The Phase 9 small-cohort cold-test (2–3 people who have not seen the project, given the TestFlight build with no instructions) is the verification artifact, not an ongoing program.

## Consequences

- **`docs/business.md` is substantially rewritten in S-0012.** Sections drop: Market Reality (10k-user analysis), Cost Model at 10k users, Seasonal Cost Profile, Grant Opportunities, Retention Paradox, Revenue Mechanisms (Explored), Acquisition as Exit Path: Khan Academy, Audience vs. Market: Community College, Non-Profit Incorporation, Syllabus Upload as Institutional Wedge. Sections reframe: Generative Graph Independence (drop the moat-for-acquisition framing — operational substance, proprietary curation through use, survives in `docs/content-strategy.md` not as a competitive position), Personal Financial Risk Ceiling → Annual Operating Subsidy Budget, Personal-Use Cost Estimates → Operating Cost Model, Standing Disposition → Personal Project Disposition with cross-link here. Cost Amplification with Session Length stays (still relevant for OQ-CONTEXT-COMPRESSION at Phase 7, regardless of disposition). New: a short Pricing and Distribution section names the cost-priced subscription, single-platform iOS via App Store IAP, no free tier, no BYOK, the $12.99/mo target, and the App Store refund window as the trial affordance. Suggested final length: ~30–40% of current.

- **`docs/MISSION.md` is substantially rewritten in S-0012.** The Audience vs. market subsection is removed — only audience remains; market disappears. Strong working commitment 2 ("Commercial sustainability without pedagogical compromise") is rewritten or absorbed: the replacement framing is "operating discipline must not corrupt pedagogy" and the threat is no longer revenue-mechanism-driven, it is builder-funnel-mechanic-driven (the builder optimizing for retention/conversion over teaching quality). The closing What this is framing is adjusted: Paideia is described honestly as a personal project the builder shipped to the iOS App Store at a cost-priced subscription, calibrated for first-encounter learners because the builder values the pedagogical discipline of that calibration, not because of market-fit. The freshman-defaults calibration survives because it protects against builder-bias drift.

- **`ROADMAP.md` Phase 4.5 / Phase 8 / Phase 9 simplify.** Phase 4.5 (input dataset survey) — license-tier framing simplifies, *consultable for personal use* is the operative bucket; *ingestable for commercial use* framing drops (nothing is consumed for commercial purposes). Phase 8 (evaluation harness) substantially simplifies — removed: external-rubric (community college instructor blind review), head-to-head against DeepTutor unmodified, OQ-PHASE8-A's three-candidate baseline-mix deliberation. Kept: Apple Developer Program enrollment + 2–4 week lead time; privacy policy authoring (consumer App Store, not institutional); cost-cap mechanism wired and tested per ADR 0029 in its narrower form; rendering-policy worked-example check per ADR 0027. Added: small private TestFlight cohort (2–3 people who haven't seen the project) as the cold-test verification of the success criterion. Phase 9 (UI prototype) narrows to iOS-only-native; Android / Google Play references removed; `cohort_id`-driven UI affordances removed; delete-account affordance added (satisfies App Store guideline 5.1.1 wired to ADR 0031's cascade); data-export affordance added (preserves cancellation-discipline honesty per commitment 6 above); static-polish discipline named explicitly; dynamic-feature foreclosure named explicitly.

- **`docs/architecture.md` Institutional Schema Provisions section is dropped in S-0012.** The institutional regime is foreclosed by this ADR; `cohort_id` is removed by [ADR 0031](0031-erasure-mechanism-and-individual-only-regime.md); the shareable-constrained-paths-as-institutional-wedge framing dies. The Syllabus Upload Pipeline's institutional product wedge framing is also dropped; the *feature* may survive as personal-use but its institutional context dies.

- **`docs/tensions.md` OQ-BYOK-REGIME closes by foreclosure.** Marked Withdrawn by ADR 0032 — both candidates excluded (institutional by the no-institutional-regime commitment; consumer by the freshman-audience calibration). OQ-WALL-BEHAVIOR survives but simplifies — single-tier soft-wall ladder under the cost-priced subscription model (no free-tier-vs-paid-tier ladder structure). OQ-CONTEXT-COMPRESSION is unaffected (still load-bearing for Phase 7, regardless of disposition). OQ-PHASE8-A loses two of its three candidates (external rubric and DeepTutor head-to-head) and becomes either "head-to-head stock Sonnet without rendering policy + small private TestFlight cohort cold-test" or closes outright; the S-0012 judgment call is made in-session.

- **The cost ceiling per [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md) survives in mechanism, reframes in justification.** Per-user cap, aggregate cap, real-time telemetry, soft-wall-degrades-don't-terminate principle — all unchanged. The ceiling's *value* is now justified as a fixed annual operating subsidy budget the builder commits in advance, not as a bridge-to-grants. The ADR 0029 *Grant timeline interacts with this commitment* consequence is retracted. ADR 0029 is not superseded — its mechanism is intact and its operating-discipline framing strengthens under this ADR. The S-0012 update to ADR 0029's text is editorial (one bullet reframed); ADR 0029's Status remains Accepted.

- **The success criterion strengthens [ADR 0012](0012-freshman-defaults-autodidact-ceiling.md) and [ADR 0027](0027-rendering-policy-prompt-layer-contract.md) — they are now load-bearing for builder-bias protection, not only for pedagogical-discipline protection.** ADR 0012's freshman calibration was originally justified by the asymmetry of failure (freshman cannot proceed; autodidact mildly annoyed). It now also serves the cold-test: the calibration target is precisely what a builder-bias-blind reader needs. ADR 0027's worked example was originally the gradeable test artifact for forbidden-token leakage at Phase 7. It now also serves as a builder-bias detector: if the builder finds the worked example natural and a cold-tester finds it baffling, the policy needs revision regardless of how the leakage check scores. Neither ADR requires text revision in S-0012 — the new load-bearing role is recorded here.

- **The corruption-vector identification generalizes.** "Leave the option open to expand later" is the seed pattern. The fix recorded here is removing the optionality, not strengthening the guard against the mechanisms the optionality preserved. Future sessions reading this ADR cold should apply the test: does this proposal preserve optionality whose value depends on a pedagogical or operational direction the builder no longer commits to? If yes, the proposal must either supersede this ADR or be foreclosed.

- **The refusal-not-deferral commitment is binding in the success case.** The most likely failure mode of a refusal-style commitment is silent re-litigation when growth makes the refused option attractive. This ADR's binding form: *if this scales beyond what the personal subsidy budget supports, the response is to raise prices to slow growth or to cap signups, not to pursue institutional clients, grants, or acquisition*. This sentence is the contract; future sessions encountering scale-pressure precedents apply it. Reopening requires a superseding ADR with explicit context for why the disposition has changed.

- **Cancellation discipline is structural.** The project may be paused or cancelled at any point. The architecture should preserve user data exportability so cancellation is honest, not destructive — Phase 9 work, not S-0012's. The data-export affordance Phase 9 success criterion above is the artifact. The discipline interacts cleanly with [ADR 0031](0031-erasure-mechanism-and-individual-only-regime.md)'s hard-delete-with-cascade — export is the user-facing capability for cancellation; cascade-delete is the user-facing capability for erasure; both are first-class.

- **This commitment does not rise to [`docs/MISSION.md`](../docs/MISSION.md)'s strong-working-commitments list.** Consistent with the [ADR 0026](0026-persistent-learner-storage-structural-not-substantive.md), [ADR 0029](0029-personal-financial-cost-ceiling.md), and [ADR 0031](0031-erasure-mechanism-and-individual-only-regime.md) precedent: this is operating discipline (project disposition), not a pedagogical commitment. Cross-references from `docs/business.md` Personal Project Disposition, `docs/MISSION.md` What this is, ROADMAP Phase 8/9, `docs/CROSS_REFERENCES.md` shared-capability consumer entry, and ENGINE_LOG are sufficient. The audience-facing pedagogical commitments survive in MISSION.md commitments 1–12; commitment 2 is rewritten to name the operating-discipline-must-not-corrupt-pedagogy framing.

- **The MemPalace decision drawer for this ADR carries the conversational reasoning verbatim.** The S-0011 exploration that surfaced the corruption vector — every "leave the option open" decision pulling design toward business outcomes — is recall-by-similarity content for future sessions facing analogous "should we keep an option open" choices. The drawer ID is recorded in the S-0012 ENGINE_LOG entry per the two-layer decision-recording discipline in CLAUDE.md.

- **Phase 1 fully closes at S-0012.** Phase 1 substantively closed at S-0011 (privacy ADRs); the supersession ADR retiring ADR 0002 plus the corresponding rewrites of `docs/business.md` and `docs/MISSION.md` complete the contract layer. Phase 2 (Build Plan Scaffolding) opens after S-0012, with the disposition above shaping every per-session contract in `build_plan/`.
