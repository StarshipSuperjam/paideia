Hanson's gestalt-perception cases (Tycho Brahe and Kepler watching the sun rise — the geocentrist sees the sun moving up; the heliocentrist sees the horizon falling away to reveal a stationary sun; X-ray images that look like meaningless gray smears to the untrained but reveal pneumonia / fluid / calcification to a trained radiologist; oscilloscope traces that look like green lines to laypeople but reveal specific waveform patterns to electrical engineers) motivate the thesis without requiring paradigm-talk. In Kuhn's framing (post-Hanson), paradigm and theory-ladenness are mutually reinforcing, but historically and conceptually theory-ladenness is the earlier and more general thesis — Kuhn extended it, did not author it. ### Finding E-6
EDGE: scientific_theory [domain=science] → scientific_model [domain=science]
   edge_type = pedagogical_prerequisite
SEP-ANCHORED REASONING: SEP entries on "Models in Science" / "The Structure of Scientific Theories" / "Theories of Scientific Theories" — the relationship between scientific theory and scientific model is intricate in contemporary philosophy of science. The semantic/model-theoretic view of theories (Suppes 1957, van Fraassen 1980, Giere 1988, Suppe 1989), which is the contemporary mainstream per the migration's own scientific_theory teaching_notes, holds that a scientific theory IS a family of models — set-theoretic structures that satisfy the theory's equations — and the theory is "applied" by claiming that a target system is similar to one of those models in relevant respects. On this view, models are not subordinate to theories but constitutive of them. The syntactic view (Carnap, Hempel, the logical positivists) — dominant mid-twentieth-century but largely collapsed under theoretical-term-to-observable-predicate linkage problems — treats theories as sets of sentences and models as interpretations of those sentences, which is closer to a genus-to-species reading. The pragmatic view (Cartwright 1983, Hacking 1983) treats theories as collections of practices, models, exemplars, and instruments. Pedagogically, on the syntactic-leaning or broad-overview reading the direction scientific_theory → scientific_model is fine: introduce theories as the central product of science, then explain models as the apparatus theories use. On the semantic-view reading (the contemporary mainstream), the relationship is more intricate — models are not downstream of theories but constitutive of them. The migration's own scientific_model teaching_notes describe the model-based view as the dominant framework. The pedagogical direction theory → model is supportable on the broad-overview reading, but the semantic view's intricate relationship may suggest a different graph structure (dual edges, or a different relation type). Sound on the broad-overview reading; closeout may judge whether the semantic-view's intricate relationship calls for a refinement (defer to closeout per master-plan §"Anti-scope" — disposition is closeout's surface).
