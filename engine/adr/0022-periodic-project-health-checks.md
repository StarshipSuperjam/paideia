# ADR 0022 — Periodic project health checks

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-04-29
- **Deciders:** S-0001 plan conversation; formalized in S-0003

## Context

A project's machinery accumulates drift. Soft-warn categories trend upward; documentation sections grow stale; conventions diverge between what `CLAUDE.md` says and what sessions actually do; tools added for one purpose linger after that purpose ends. The accumulation is invisible turn-by-turn — no single session is responsible — but compounds across many sessions into structural debt that is expensive to repay later.

Two approaches are available. **Reactive** — fix things when they're noticed in the course of other work. This is what most projects do; it produces uneven coverage (busy areas get attention; quiet areas drift). **Periodic** — schedule explicit audit sessions whose job is to assess fit, gaps, dead weight, and bloat across the whole machinery, on a known cadence.

Periodic audits exploit two facts. First, they have a different posture than feature work — looking *for* problems rather than *at* them in passing. Second, they consume telemetry that's been quietly accumulating: `tools/validate-history.jsonl` (per-category soft-warn trends), `session/archive/S-NNNN.json` (per-session outcome summaries), ADR status field (counts of Accepted / Deprecated / Superseded over time), ENGINE_LOG entries (categorized engine changes by date), MemPalace `exploration` and `decision` tags (semantic memory of every conversation). The telemetry exists to be consumed; without a scheduled consumer, it accumulates without surfacing signal.

## Decision

The project runs **periodic health checks** at a configurable cadence (default every 10 sessions as of S-0033 — was every 30 from S-0001 to S-0032; tightened by user direction at S-0032 because the S-0032 MemPalace audit surfaced enough silent failures, see Consequences amendment below). The cadence trigger fires automatically at session boot when `(next_id - last_audit_session) >= health_check_cadence` (overdue-catchup logic introduced at S-0041 — see Consequences amendment below; the prior strict-modulo logic `next_id mod health_check_cadence == 0` silently slid the trigger forward by a full cadence whenever the cadence-aligned slot was consumed by user-directed work). The trigger fires both at the natural-cadence slot (`slots_since == cadence`, "due") and at every later slot (`slots_since > cadence`, "overdue") until the audit fires. The user accepts (the session's work is the audit, per [`engine/operations/health-check.md`](../operations/health-check.md); `engine/tools/health_check.py` bumps `last_audit_session` at report-emit time, clearing the trigger) or defers (proceed with planned work; trigger fires again next session).

Health checks are not phase-anchored. They fall wherever the modulus or overdue condition lands; the audit consumes whatever telemetry has accumulated since the last check.

## Consequences

- The cadence machinery is built in S-0001 (`engine/session/register_state.json` carries `health_check_cadence`; the boot procedure computes the modulus). The health-check session itself runs from [`engine/operations/health-check.md`](../operations/health-check.md) (categories, report template).
- [`engine/tools/health_check.py`](../tools/health_check.py) **landed at S-0029** under the ADR 0038 three-layer code-discipline contract; the deliverable handshake per [`engine/operations/cascade-discipline.md`](../operations/cascade-discipline.md) closes the original anticipation in this entry. Trend canon for the script is the structured `outcome_summary_soft_warns` field in committed session archives, per [ADR 0042](0042-soft-warn-lifecycle-archive-canon.md). **First check landed at S-0030** as a manual phase-boundary fire (see [`docs/health-checks/S-0030.md`](../../docs/health-checks/S-0030.md)).
- The cadence-trigger logic was originally specified as `last_claimed mod health_check_cadence == 0`. The S-0030 audit measured this as off-by-one against this ADR's prose intent ("first check expected around S-0030"): the original logic strictly fires at the boot of the session AFTER the cadence-numbered session. **S-0031 corrected the logic to `next_id mod health_check_cadence == 0`** — the slot about to be claimed — and amended this Decision section accordingly per [ADR 0043](0043-hook-architecture.md). The corrected logic was then mirrored in `.claude/commands/start-engine.md`, `engine/operations/health-check.md`, and the new SessionStart hook script `engine/tools/hooks/session-start.sh`.

- **S-0041 amendment: strict-modulo replaced by overdue-catchup.** The S-0031 strict-modulo logic introduced a second failure mode: when the cadence-aligned slot was consumed by user-directed work (e.g., S-0040 was claimed for the deferred MemPalace wing-filter fix per S-0040's `outcome_summary`), the cadence trigger silently slid forward by a full cadence — the next firing landed at S-0050, a 19-session gap nearly 2x the configured 10-session cadence. The slide is invisible at the trigger surface itself (the hook just reports "no trigger this session") and produces no archive-side soft-warn. S-0041 boot caught the slide because the user explicitly asked "this session is one past health check; why is it not engaging?" — the discovery surface was AI-noticing-after-being-prompted, not mechanical-detection. The fix replaces strict-modulo with overdue-catchup tracking: `register_state.json` gains a `last_audit_session` field (bootstrap value `S-0030`, the first audit); `engine/tools/health_check.py` bumps it at report-emit time; the SessionStart hook computes `slots_since = next_id - last_audit_session` and fires when `slots_since >= cadence` (with separate "due" and "overdue" surface text); `engine/tools/validate.py` adds a `health_check_overdue` soft-warn for defense-in-depth so a silently-failing hook (the S-0033/S-0034 vector pattern) cannot mask the slide. Carriers updated in this session: this ADR Decision + Consequences, `engine/adr/0043-hook-architecture.md` Consequences (which referenced the strict-modulo logic), `engine/operations/health-check.md`, `engine/operations/session-build-lifecycle.md` and its mirroring skill, `engine/operations/tools-validate-interpretation.md` (new soft-warn entry), `.claude/commands/start-engine.md`. The legacy strict-modulo path is preserved in the SessionStart hook as a fallback when `last_audit_session` is absent (legacy `register_state.json`); the fallback emits a `last-audit-absent-falling-back-to-strict-modulo` log line so the regression is visible.
- Audit categories (per `docs/operations/health-check.md`): soft-warn trend analysis, ADR status drift, ENGINE_LOG cadence, doc/code consistency, MemPalace coverage gaps, `tensions.md` aging, file-size and complexity heuristics.
- Defer is a first-class option. If the cadence fires during a busy phase (e.g., mid-Phase 5 seed authoring with 6+ subdomain sessions in flight), the user defers; the trigger re-fires at the next satisfied modulus. Audits are not blocking.
- The audit produces a report (template in `docs/operations/health-check.md`) and one or more ENGINE_LOG entries (`Changed`/`Removed`/`Added`) for any cleanups landed in the audit session. Findings that warrant follow-up become explicit work items in `STATE.md`.
- The cadence is **configurable**, not hardcoded. As the project matures, the right cadence may change (longer interval as machinery stabilizes; shorter interval if telemetry shows fast drift).
- **S-0032 amendment: cadence tightened from 30 → 10.** The S-0032 MemPalace audit surfaced multiple silent failures that had accumulated unobserved across the cadence-30 window: (1) the `mempalace` CLI was not on the hook subshell's PATH, so the Stop/PreCompact wrapper had been silently writing FAIL exit=127 since S-0002 (485 manual-MCP drawers exist; zero hook-driven captures); (2) the S-0030 audit's headline "1 decision drawer for 42 ADRs / 2.4% compliance" was inflated by ~7x because it counted by room name only and missed `decision`-tagged drawers in the legacy `general` room; (3) the post-S-0030 ADR-companion backfill into the `decisions` room produced a parallel filing pattern that stayed silently inconsistent for ~20 ADRs. None of these failures were structurally invisible — each could have been caught by an ops audit — but the 30-session cadence meant they only surfaced at the user's manual phase-boundary fire (S-0030) or the explicit user-directed audit (S-0032). User direction at S-0032: tighten to 10. Raise back when the project demonstrates more consistent execution. Forward refrence: first cadence-10 fire lands at S-0040; after-that S-0050, S-0060, etc. The cadence_history field in `engine/session/register_state.json` records the change with rationale.
- **S-0097 amendment: cadence raised from 10 → 20.** User direction at S-0097 boot, applied in the same session as the deferred cadence-fired audit (the formal first exercise of [ADR 0057](0057-adversarial-stance-for-health-check-audits.md) per the readiness note at [`engine/build_readiness/adversarial_stance_first_exercise.md`](../build_readiness/adversarial_stance_first_exercise.md); the trigger fired at S-0087 boot and was deferred through C1–C7 of the issue close-out campaign). Reason: routine sessions (per [ADR 0051](0051-routine-mode-and-engine-loop.md)) consume slots in 8–12-session batches executing similar tasks against a single target — `T-PHASE-5-AUDIT` is the proximate example with 12 routine sessions producing largely-converged telemetry across the batch (one cross-bridge census + 10 per-subdomain audits + one hubs/traces audit, all reading from the same Phase-5 graph). Cadence-10 was sized when slot-count tracked roughly with telemetry-variety (mostly heterogeneous interactive build sessions); under the routine model, a 10-session window can elapse with most archives belonging to a single batch. A 20-session window better captures ≥1 routine batch plus several interactive sessions, producing a more diverse audit-input pool. The raise is consistent with the canonical `If health checks consistently produce no actionable findings — raise the cadence (e.g., 10 → 20 → 30)` policy in [`engine/operations/health-check.md`](../operations/health-check.md), but the operative reason here is *signal heterogeneity per cadence window* rather than *low actionable finding density*. Forward reference: under cadence-20, the next cadence fire will land at `slots_since >= 20` after `last_audit_session: S-0097` — i.e., S-0117. Adjust further if subsequent audits show drift accumulating between checks (lower) or audits consistently producing no actionable findings (raise further). The `health_check_cadence_history` field in `engine/session/register_state.json` records the change with rationale.
- Deferring health checks indefinitely is a self-inflicted cost. The cadence trigger is an interrupt, not a request — the user should accept it unless there's an active reason to defer.
