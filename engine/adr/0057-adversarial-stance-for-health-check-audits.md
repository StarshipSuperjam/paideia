# ADR 0057 — Adversarial stance for project health-check audits

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-05-06
- **Deciders:** S-0085 (issue close-out campaign session S-C; closes Issues #30, #28, #36)

## Context

[ADR 0022](0022-periodic-project-health-checks.md) commits the project to periodic audits — a different posture from feature work, *looking for* problems rather than at them in passing — on a configurable cadence (default every 10 sessions as of S-0033). [`engine/operations/health-check.md`](../operations/health-check.md) carries the operative-discipline framing: *"is this thing doing the work it was created to do, or is it plumbing waiting for a function that never arrived?"* and the four traditional postures (Fit / Gaps / Infrastructure-without-function / Bloat) the audit applies.

The framing is correct in principle. In practice, six audits across the project's lifetime (S-0030, S-0041, S-0052, S-0065, S-0077) reveal a structural failure mode the framing does not prevent: the audit's prose mechanism defaults to *confirming* that things still work, with **preserve** as the safe disposition. Across cumulative audit windows the preserve-disposition reinforces itself — S-0052 said preserve; S-0065 cited S-0052; S-0077 cited both. The compounding cheapness produces accumulated dead-weight no audit retires, because no audit has the posture-budget to argue for retirement.

The failure mode surfaced concretely at S-0077 (the most recent audit, the first dominated by sustained routine-mode execution). [Issue #28](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/28) (stats-as-proxy-for-function) and [Issue #29](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/29) (`product/docs/ideation.md` superseded register) were both caught at S-0077 — but only because the user surfaced them at session close, not because the audit's prose mechanism worked. The mechanism would have continued to preserve `ideation.md` and trust MemPalace drawer counts indefinitely, audit after audit. S-0065's MemPalace observation cited a cached drawer count from S-0032 STATE.md and treated existence as a healthy signal; meanwhile, the routine-mode-skips-diary-write gap ([Issue #27](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/27)) was actively dropping `pushback`/`lesson` capture across every Phase 5 routine session — invisible to drawer-count-as-stat but obvious to anyone who tried to *use* MemPalace recall during routine work.

Three issues frame the same gap from three angles:

- [Issue #30](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/30) names the missing posture inversion: *adversarial stance*, paralleling [ADR 0040](0040-build-readiness-gate-before-substantive-build-sessions.md)'s gate-side adversarial reconnaissance discipline. ADR 0040 asks *"what could break our session?"* prospectively; the audit-side equivalent asks *"what is broken or stale or superseded that we've been carrying as if it were fine?"* retrospectively.
- [Issue #28](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/28) names the concrete failure mode: stats-as-proxy-for-function. Counts, status fields, and existence checks do not answer the operative diagnostic question; they only say the system exists. The fix is content probes at audit-time, not citations of cached stats.
- [Issue #36](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/36) names the specific signal the audit has been ignoring: the `pushback`-tagged and `lesson`-tagged drawers in MemPalace. Those tags exist precisely to be retrieved later — accumulated complaints and learnings — and the audit is the natural consumer. The current operative discipline asks soft questions like "are MemPalace queries surfacing useful prior context?" but never directs the audit to *read* the actual drawers.

The structural diagnosis matches the *compound-drift* mode [ADR 0040](0040-build-readiness-gate-before-substantive-build-sessions.md) names at the session level and [ADR 0038](0038-expression-contract-for-ai-authored-code.md) names at the code level: layered gates that check artifact-vs-contract alignment, not contract-vs-reality alignment. An audit that confirms each artifact still holds its declared shape — declares the same status, has the same inbound references, fits the same posture bucket — passes its own layered checks even when the artifact's *function* migrated elsewhere or evaporated. The fix at code-level (ADR 0038's three layers) and at session-level (ADR 0040's gate posture) is *adversarial framing* — argue against the status quo; only preserve with affirmative case. The audit needs the same.

## Decision

The project authors an **adversarial stance** for periodic health-check audits — a posture inversion that runs across the audit's existing operative-discipline frame. The operational surface lives at [`../operations/health-check.md`](../operations/health-check.md). This ADR is the citable contract.

The adversarial stance commits five contract elements:

**1. User-buffered execution.** The audit's deliverable is **findings + guidance suggestions, surfaced to the user, before any action is taken**. The audit *reports* and *recommends*; the user *adjudicates and authorizes*; downstream sessions execute approved actions per [ADR 0048](0048-handoff-narrowing-and-github-issues-for-cross-session-deferrals.md) issue-discipline (the new `health-check-finding` label captures audit-spawned Issues; HANDOFF.md is reserved for next-session-must-resolve handoffs). This is the structural counterpart to [ADR 0040](0040-build-readiness-gate-before-substantive-build-sessions.md)'s "gate sessions are conversational by default" posture — the cadence audit is conversational by default in the same way. The audit session has no path to executing retire / convert / replace actions autonomously; "auto-resolve" of an audit finding is a misidentification of the session's mode. Inline trivial cleanups that fall under the standard "default to fix-in-context" rule still apply — the buffer is for the audit's *adversarial recommendations* (retirement, replacement, structural change), not for stray typos noticed in passing.

**2. Posture inversion is load-bearing.** The four traditional postures (Fit / Gaps / Infrastructure-without-function / Bloat) get adversarial prompt-question rewrites baked into the report template. Each posture is reframed from "confirm it works" to "argue it should be retired or replaced; only preserve with affirmative case." The disposition labels for Infrastructure-without-function shift from `[retire / convert / preserve]` to `[recommend retire / recommend convert / recommend preserve-with-affirmative-case]` — recommendation-shape per element 1, and the affirmative case must specify *what work this artifact does that no other artifact does*. "Inbound references" alone is not an affirmative case; the references are themselves candidates for retirement.

**3. Stats-as-proxy-for-function is a named anti-pattern.** Any system reference in the audit must specify what *content probe* runs against it. Counts, status fields, and existence checks do not answer the operative diagnostic question — they only say the system exists. Every system the audit touches gets a fresh content probe at audit-time, not a citation of cached stats. The probe surfaces live in [`../operations/health-check.md`](../operations/health-check.md) "Mechanical inputs and freshness probes" — MemPalace search recall, validator soft-warn acted-on rate, Supabase migration empirical postcondition, hook-log success-exit verification, register-content capture rate. The contract: the audit's prose claim about a system's health must rest on a probe run *during this audit*, not on a cited number from a prior audit or a cached entry in `engine/STATE.md`.

**4. Required output is ≥1 affirmative retire-candidate-recommendation.** The current discipline (per `health-check.md` "operative diagnostic") only requires ≥1 non-obvious finding. The new contract adds ≥1 retire-candidate-with-reasoning *as a surfaced recommendation*, not an executed action. An audit that ends with all preserves and zero retires is structurally suspicious in the same way an audit producing no findings would be — it indicates the adversarial posture wasn't exercised. The escape hatch: if the project's current state honestly admits no retire candidates, the audit authors an explicit "no retire candidates this audit" subsection that adversarially scrutinizes its own claim ("I argue no retire candidates because X; if X were false, Y would be a candidate"). The escape is open *and* answerable — it prevents fabricated retires while preserving the surfacing pressure.

**5. Cold-context probe per audit.** The audit reads ≥1 randomly-selected artifact (operations doc, ADR, register, build-plan chunk, STATE.md row, hook script) *as if* it had no project context, and asks: do cross-references resolve to currently-correct content? does the prose tell a future cold consumer how to *use* this artifact, or only that it exists? does the artifact name a sibling that no longer exists? does it carry a rule whose successor superseded it without a back-reference? The cold-context probe is the audit's defense against compound drift in artifacts the warm-context audit can't see — the audit knows too much, and that knowledge is itself the failure mode. A randomly-selected target prevents the audit from converging on artifacts it already trusts.

The contract's *amendment discipline* mirrors [ADR 0040](0040-build-readiness-gate-before-substantive-build-sessions.md)'s. Refinements within the contract are cheap because they preserve the discipline; restructuring the contract itself is expensive because it is what the discipline binds. Refining the freshness-probe inventory (adding a new system, sharpening a probe, replacing one probe with a better one), refining the cold-context random-pick procedure, sharpening the "affirmative case" criterion for preserves, adjusting the cluster-detection guidance for `pushback`/`lesson` replay — all ENGINE_LOG-tracked refinements to [`../operations/health-check.md`](../operations/health-check.md) and [`../../docs/health-checks/TEMPLATE.md`](../../docs/health-checks/TEMPLATE.md). Removing the user-buffered execution principle, dropping the ≥1-retire-candidate requirement, retiring the cold-context probe, making preserve the default again — posture changes requiring a superseding ADR.

The contract's *first exercise* per [ADR 0053](0053-mechanism-first-exercise-gate.md): the cadence-fired audit at S-0087 (campaign session S-E by recommended sequencing per [`~/.claude/plans/i-need-a-plan-prancy-ripple.md`](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/30)). First-exercise readiness note authored at [`../build_readiness/adversarial_stance_first_exercise.md`](../build_readiness/adversarial_stance_first_exercise.md). The S-0087 audit is the first that exercises the new posture end-to-end; if friction surfaces (e.g., the cold-context probe randomly picks an artifact that resists the cold read for legitimate reasons), the operations doc takes the refinement, not the contract.

## Consequences

- **The operational document is the working surface; this ADR is the citable contract.** [`../operations/health-check.md`](../operations/health-check.md) carries the adversarial-stance prose, the freshness-probe inventory per external system, the inverted posture prompt-questions, the `pushback`/`lesson` drawer-query procedure, and the report-template guidance. [`../../docs/health-checks/TEMPLATE.md`](../../docs/health-checks/TEMPLATE.md) carries the report shape sessions populate. Refinements land there under ENGINE_LOG tracking; posture changes require superseding this ADR. The same separation [ADR 0036](0036-expression-contract-for-inward-documents.md), [ADR 0038](0038-expression-contract-for-ai-authored-code.md), [ADR 0039](0039-universal-expression-contract-across-ai-authoring-patterns.md), and [ADR 0040](0040-build-readiness-gate-before-substantive-build-sessions.md) maintain between themselves and their operational surfaces.

- **Closes Issues #30, #28, #36 with one ADR.** [Issue #30](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/30) sequenced itself as structurally upstream of #28 and #36 — closing the adversarial-stance contract closes the umbrella, and the freshness-probe inventory (#28) and `pushback`/`lesson` drawer-query (#36) land as concrete instances of the umbrella in the operational surface. The three Issues are intentionally clustered: any one without the others would have been incomplete (a freshness-probe inventory without the adversarial posture is a sharper audit running on the same defensive-preserve track; a `pushback`/`lesson` query without the user-buffered-execution principle invites the audit to act on cluster findings autonomously).

- **The audit's auto-mode interrupt criteria invert** (matching [ADR 0040](0040-build-readiness-gate-before-substantive-build-sessions.md)'s gate-session inversion). [CLAUDE.md](../../CLAUDE.md)'s standing rules name escalation criteria for sessions running autonomously: irreversible-with-unclear-path, unsolvable, destructive-action-confirmation. Adversarial-stance audits are *conversational by default* — their job is to surface findings and resolve them with the user, not to push through autonomously. An audit that finds itself wanting to "auto-resolve" a Tier 1 finding (retire a doc, replace a register, restructure a contract) has misidentified its mode. The audit's deliverable is co-authored with the user: the AI surfaces, the user directs, downstream sessions execute via Issues per [ADR 0048](0048-handoff-narrowing-and-github-issues-for-cross-session-deferrals.md).

- **The S-0087 audit is the first exercise.** Per [ADR 0053](0053-mechanism-first-exercise-gate.md). The cadence trigger fires at S-0087 (`slots_since == 10` since `last_audit_session: S-0077`); recommended campaign sequencing per [`~/.claude/plans/i-need-a-plan-prancy-ripple.md`](https://github.com/StarshipSuperjam/paideia/issues/30) coincides S-0087 with campaign session S-E (`apply_migration` postcondition verification, Issue #23). Either the audit consumes S-0087 alone or interleaves with S-E per the user's adjudication at boot. The first-exercise readiness note at [`../build_readiness/adversarial_stance_first_exercise.md`](../build_readiness/adversarial_stance_first_exercise.md) tracks readiness criteria; closes when the S-0087 (or any subsequent first-exercise audit) lands a hand-authored report under the new TEMPLATE.md shape with non-empty Freshness probes, Accumulated pushbacks/lessons, and Affirmative retire candidates subsections, plus an empty-on-arrival User adjudication subsection.

- **No retroactive grandfathering.** The new contract takes force at acceptance. Prior audit reports (S-0030, S-0041, S-0052, S-0065, S-0077) do not require retrofitting. The contract applies forward from the next cadence-fired audit. The S-0077 hand-authored report shape is preserved as a transitional reference for the new template.

- **No code changes to [`../tools/health_check.py`](../tools/health_check.py).** The script's existing `audit_mempalace()` function (engine/tools/health_check.py:1039) already runs `pushback`/`lesson` adoption-count probes (added between S-0065 and S-0077). The contract documents these as canonical contract in the ops doc; no new probes added. If S-0087's first exercise surfaces a gap that warrants script-level mechanization (e.g., automated cluster-detection across the `pushback`/`lesson` window), that's a follow-on Issue under the audit's own posture, not a precondition for the contract's force. The script's auto-generated report sections continue to feed mechanical inputs; the audit AI overwrites the script output with hand-authored prose per the existing "the report is the byproduct; the audit's value lives in the prose" discipline.

- **Cluster detection for `pushback`/`lesson` drawers is AI-judgment work at audit time, not a script.** Per Issue #36's scope notes: the audit AI runs `mempalace_search` against the `pushback` and `lesson` tags scoped to drawers since `last_audit_session`, reads each drawer's verbatim content, and surfaces clusters in prose. Multiple pushbacks against the same risk-class → posture rule that hasn't been mechanized → recommendation for new ADR or validator check. Multiple lessons against the same workflow → ops-doc gap → recommendation for ops-doc update or new validator check. The audit produces concrete recommendations; the user adjudicates per element 1 of the Decision.

- **Plays well with the four standing dispositions** in [`../operations/health-check.md`](../operations/health-check.md)'s "Report template" guidance: corrective actions either land as inline follow-on commits (small scope-bounded fixes), become next-session work items in [`../STATE.md`](../STATE.md), become new GitHub Issues with `health-check-finding` label per [ADR 0048](0048-handoff-narrowing-and-github-issues-for-cross-session-deferrals.md), or become tensions in [`../../product/docs/tensions.md`](../../product/docs/tensions.md). Element 1 (user-buffered execution) constrains *which* actions land inline — only trivial-cleanup-noticed-in-passing per the "default to fix-in-context" rule. Adversarial recommendations (retire, convert, restructure) route through the user adjudication subsection, then to one of the other three dispositions per the user's choice.

- **Refinement under load is expected.** S-0087 is the first real test. The freshness-probe inventory will sharpen with use (some probes will turn out to be stats-as-proxy in disguise; some systems will turn out to need a different probe shape). The cold-context random-pick procedure may surface artifacts whose cold-read failure is legitimate (e.g., a register file that intentionally lives empty as plumbing for a future capture). These are refinement signals — ENGINE_LOG-tracked — not contract failures. If the contract produces friction disproportionate to the compound-drift it prevents, the contract gets revised under its own amendment discipline.

- **Sibling structural relationship to ADR 0040.** Both contracts apply the *adversarial framing* pattern at different temporal scales: [ADR 0040](0040-build-readiness-gate-before-substantive-build-sessions.md) at the gate before a substantive build session opens (prospective; "what could break our session?"); this ADR at the cadence audit over accumulated state (retrospective; "what's broken or stale that we've been carrying as if it were fine?"). Both contracts inherit "conversational by default" — their job is to surface decisions and resolve them with the user. Both rely on operational documents for refinement and on superseding ADRs for posture change. Neither contract's posture is enforceable by validator alone; the discipline is held by AI-authoring-with-the-user-in-the-loop. Mechanical surfaces (validator soft-warns, the dead-weight scanner, `health_check.py`'s script-generated report) are inputs to the audit, not substitutes for it.
